# Immigration main-question reset

Date: 2026-04-10

## Why the old question is wrong

`Is low-skill immigration bad for a country?` is too coarse to carry the evidence.

Reason:
1. It collapses `federal`, `state/local`, `native-low-skill`, `renters`, `employers`, and `future descendants` into one ledger. [SOURCE: https://www.cbo.gov/system/files/2025-06/61256-immigration-state-local.pdf]
2. It hides horizon. A `1-5 year` local-capacity question is not the same as a `30-75 year` descendant-fiscal question. [SOURCE: https://d279m997dpfwgl.cloudfront.net/wp/2016/09/0922_immigrant-economics-full-report.pdf]
3. It hides counterfactuals. If `AGI soon` is live, the relevant comparison is not just against status quo demographics but also against automation, pronatalism, offshoring, internal mobility, and tighter enforcement. [SOURCE: https://www.imf.org/-/media/files/research/imf-and-g20/2025/g20-background-note-on-aging-and-migration.pdf]
4. It hides geography. Our own warehouse is strongest on `origin x destination x household-type` heterogeneity, not on one national scalar. [SOURCE: research/immigration-epistemic-check.md]

## Canonical question

Use this instead:

`Under explicit horizons and counterfactuals, which low-skill immigration flows, into which destinations, improve or degrade institutional resilience, local-system capacity, and net citizen welfare, and on which ledger?`

Short version:

`For which origin x destination x household cells is low-skill immigration federally positive, locally negative, negative on both margins, or politically destabilizing?`

AGI-soon version:

`Given uncertainty about transformative automation between 2026 and 2040, which immigration margins reduce short-run transition risk and improve national option value, after accounting for local capacity costs, aging and care needs, political backlash, and the possibility that labor inflows either complement or delay automation?` [SOURCE: https://www.imf.org/-/media/files/research/imf-and-g20/2025/g20-background-note-on-aging-and-migration.pdf] [SOURCE: https://docs.iza.org/dp18218.pdf]

## Working rules for future claims

1. If a claim is about `national growth` or `federal budget`, do not use local housing or school proxies as if they settle it.
2. If a claim is about `local burden`, do not use national GDP or average-consumer gains as if they erase it.
3. If a claim is about `descendants`, say whether it is NAS-style long horizon or short-run transition.
4. If a claim is about `country-level good/bad`, replace it with an incidence table.
5. If a claim depends on AGI timing, mark it `[FRAMING-SENSITIVE]`.

## Immediate consequence

The project should now be read as an `incidence-and-capacity mapping project` with partial fiscal evidence, not as a completed proof that low-skill immigration is nationally net good or nationally net bad. [SOURCE: research/immigration-epistemic-check.md]
