# Immigration — Dataset Roadmap, Batch 5: Direct-Cost / Welfare-Admin / Niche (2026-06-24)

Fifth batch. Supplements the **45 datasets** already proposed across
`immigration-dataset-roadmap.md` (12), `-additions-2026-06-24.md` (6), `-batch3-2026-06-24.md` (14+3),
`-batch4-us-2026-06-24.md` (7), `-batch4-intl-2026-06-24.md` (6), and the held stack in
`immigration-dataset-register.md`. **None of these repeats the 45 already on the books.**

**Axis = direct cost / welfare-admin / niche.** Gaps targeted, in priority order requested by parent:
(1) **welfare admin by citizenship** — TANF (ACF caseload), WIC (FNS), SSI (SSA), HUD Picture of
Subsidized Households — direct program-use *cost* data with a citizenship field;
(2) **fertility / birthright** — NCHS Natality / NVSS births by maternal nativity (the birthright-citizenship
fiscal channel);
(3) **substance / deviance** — NSDUH (substance use + criminal-behavior self-report by nativity);
(4) **DACA outcomes** — NURP / DACA-recipient outcome surveys (vs the USCIS active-recipient *counts* already listed);
(5) **gangs / recidivism** — National Gang Center, BJS recidivism series.

All URLs/DOIs verified live via search 2026-06-24 unless tagged `[UNVERIFIED]`.

Join-key legend for the warehouse: `state_fips`, `county_fips`, `POBP`/origin-country, `education_bucket`,
`citizenship`/`nativity`, `topic`, `FY/year`. A dataset with no nativity/citizenship field is flagged as an
ecological/calibration layer, not a status dataset — and (this batch's recurring theme) several federal
**welfare-admin** systems record an *alien/citizenship-eligibility* field that ordinary surveys lack, which is
exactly what makes them high-value here.

## Axis 1 — Welfare admin by citizenship (highest priority; direct program-use cost with a citizenship field)

### SSI-ASR-NONCITIZEN — SSA Supplemental Security Income, recipients by citizenship status
**Source:** Social Security Administration, Office of Research, Evaluation & Statistics (ORES) · **Access:** PUBLIC — annual report, HTML + downloadable "all tables" workbook (~0.7 MB), no registration; scriptable from stable `ssa.gov/policy/docs/statcomps/ssi_asr/<year>/` paths.
**Official:** report hub https://www.ssa.gov/policy/docs/statcomps/ssi_asr/index.html · 2024 noncitizens section (Tables 29 + 30) https://www.ssa.gov/policy/docs/statcomps/ssi_asr/2024/sect05.html · 2024 index + downloads https://www.ssa.gov/policy/docs/statcomps/ssi_asr/2024/index.html · noncitizen-eligibility rules (POMS SI 00502.100, the 7 "qualified alien" categories) https://secure.ssa.gov/POMS.nsf/lnx/0500502100 [SOURCE: ssa.gov/policy/docs/statcomps/ssi_asr/2024/sect05.html]
**Format / size:** annual HTML tables + a combined tables workbook per year; long back-series (Table 29 noncitizen recipients **1982–2024**). SSI program total = **~$63 B/yr**, 7.4 M recipients (Dec 2024).
**Key variables — DOES IT HAVE CITIZENSHIP? YES, explicitly.** **Table 29** = noncitizen SSI recipients by eligibility category (aged / blind-disabled), annual 1982→ with noncitizen share of all recipients. **Table 30** = "Recipients, by selected characteristics **and citizenship status**" — Citizens vs Noncitizens cross-tabbed by age (and the supplement adds state). Program-wide: federally-administered payments, average monthly payment ($697), state supplementation. The companion **Annual Statistical Supplement §7.E** adds recipient characteristics; SSI federal-payment-by-state tables give the geography.
**Why it fits:** the **direct federal-administrative count of noncitizen cash-welfare receipt** — a real cost-side ledger line for the aged/disabled-immigrant SSI channel, with a citizenship split ACS/CPS self-reports only approximate. The noncitizen share fell from ~12% (1995) to ~4.4% (2024) post-PRWORA — itself a policy-relevant trend. Pairs with the held SSA actuarial note (contributions side) to bracket the SSA-program net position for noncitizens.
**Limit / honesty:** "noncitizen" = SSA's qualified-alien eligibility categories (refugees, LPRs, asylees, paroled, etc.) — it is a **lawfully-present** count, NOT the unauthorized (the unauthorized are statutorily ineligible for SSI, so they are absent by construction — a feature for cost-attribution honesty, not a bug). Aggregate report tables, not microdata (true SSI microdata = restricted). Table 30's finest geography is the supplement's state tables, not county. No origin-country detail. [SOURCE: ssa.gov/policy/docs/statcomps/ssi_asr/2024/index.html]
**Graph fit:** `welfare_admin` cost layer; `citizenship` (citizen/noncitizen) × eligibility-category × year (× state via supplement); national + state; joins the state-local cost ledger by `state_fips`; no origin join.

### TANF-CHARACTERISTICS — ACF Characteristics & Financial Circumstances of TANF Recipients, by citizenship
**Source:** HHS Administration for Children & Families, Office of Family Assistance (OFA) · **Access:** PUBLIC — annual XLSX (+ PDF) per FY, no registration; scriptable from stable `acf.gov/sites/default/files/documents/ofa/fy<NN>_characteristics.xlsx` paths (also mirrored on data.gov).
**Official:** data hub https://acf.gov/ofa/programs/tanf/data-reports · FY2024 release (XLSX, 514 KB) https://acf.gov/ofa/data/characteristics-and-financial-circumstances-tanf-recipients-fiscal-year-2024 · FY2022 table list (PDF, shows Tables 23 + 35) https://acf.gov/sites/default/files/documents/ofa/fy2022_characteristics.pdf · TANF Data Report instructions (data element #42/#76 = Citizenship/Alienage) https://acf.gov/sites/default/files/documents/ofa/tanf_data_reports_tan_ssp_instructions_definitions.pdf · data.gov catalog https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/characteristics-and-financial-circumstances-of-tanf-recipients [SOURCE: acf.gov/sites/default/files/documents/ofa/fy2022_characteristics.pdf]
**Format / size:** one Excel workbook per FY (~370–515 KB), national + **state-level** tables, FY2017→ on data.gov (earlier in archives). Underlying TANF Data Report is the disaggregated case-level admin collection (states report monthly; OFA tabulates).
**Key variables — DOES IT HAVE CITIZENSHIP? YES, explicitly.** **Table 23 "TANF Adult Recipients by Citizenship Status"** and **Table 35 "TANF Child Recipients by Citizenship Status"** — the published cross-tabs. Plus: family size, child-only vs adult cases (40.9% are child-only — relevant to the citizen-child / noncitizen-parent "mixed-status" channel), age, race/ethnicity, **educational attainment** (37% of adults <HS — maps to the project's low-skill focus), employment status, cash-assistance amount ($673/mo avg), non-TANF income, reasons for case closure. The case-level TANF Data Report records data element **#42/#76 Citizenship/Alienage** (with an "Unknown" code permitted only for non-eligible-family members).
**Why it fits:** the **direct federal-administrative TANF cash-welfare count with a citizenship field** — the cost-side counterpart to ITEP's tax estimate for the cash-assistance program, with both adult AND child citizenship (the child tables matter because the birthright-citizen-child-of-immigrant pathway is exactly the fiscal channel the fertility axis below tracks). State + national, annual, free XLSX — directly scriptable beside the existing CBO/EOIR parse jobs but cleaner (already tabular).
**Limit / honesty:** published tables are aggregates (case-level TANF Data Report microdata is restricted via NDACAN DUA). "Citizenship Status" categories follow the ACF coding (U.S. citizen / qualified alien / etc.); the unauthorized are largely ineligible for federal TANF (so, like SSI, mostly absent by construction — though state-funded SSP-MOE programs and the citizen children of unauthorized parents complicate "who benefits"). Child-only cases mean caseload ≠ adult-benefit count. State responsibility/eligibility rules vary (TANF is a block grant). [SOURCE: acf.gov/ofa/programs/tanf/data-reports]
**Graph fit:** `welfare_admin` cost layer; `citizenship` (adult + child, separately) × `state_fips` × FY; joins the state-local cost ledger; pairs with SSI-ASR (cash welfare) and the proposed CMS Emergency-Medicaid / stage5 SNAP layers; no origin join.

### HUD-PSH / HUD-PUMS — Picture of Subsidized Households + HUD Tenant PUMS (housing-assistance use; citizenship via the microdata, NOT the Picture aggregates)
**Source:** HUD Office of Policy Development & Research (HUD USER) · **Access:** PUBLIC — Picture summary files + custom-query tool (no registration, scriptable bulk CSV/ASCII); HUD Tenant **PUMS** = downloadable 5% tenant-record microdata.
**Official:** Picture hub https://www.huduser.gov/portal/datasets/assthsg.html · Picture 2024 data dictionary https://www.huduser.gov/portal/datasets/pictures/dictionary_2024.pdf · HUD PUMS data dictionary (v17) https://www.huduser.gov/apps/public/pums/download_pdf/PUMS-Data-Dictionary.pdf · data.gov catalog https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/picture-of-subsidized-households · CRS "Noncitizen Eligibility for Federal Housing Programs" (R46462, the policy frame) https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/R46462 [SOURCE: huduser.gov/portal/datasets/assthsg.html]
**Format / size:** Picture = annual summary files at U.S./state/PHA/**CBSA/tract/county/ZIP/city/congressional-district** levels (11 summary levels), ~5 M assisted households, programs split (Public Housing, **Housing Choice Vouchers**, Project-Based S8, Mod-Rehab, 202/811, etc.). PUMS = 5% stratified sample of tenant certification records (50 states + DC + PR), from IMS/PIC + TRACS, most records ≤18 months old.
**Key variables — DOES IT HAVE CITIZENSHIP? NOT on the Picture aggregates; only via the microdata.** **VERIFIED in the 2024 Picture data dictionary: the Picture summary file carries program, geography, units, %occupied, average income & income source, and %-distributions of age/disability/race-ethnicity — but NO citizenship/immigration field.** Tenant citizenship ("eligible noncitizen" / "ineligible noncitizen" / mixed-status proration under Section 214) lives on the underlying **Form HUD-50058 / HUD-50059** records and is surfaced (where disclosable) in the **HUD Tenant PUMS**, not the Picture tables. So the housing-assistance-by-citizenship question is answerable only through the PUMS microdata or a custom restricted extract — NOT the headline Picture product.
**Why it fits:** housing assistance (vouchers + public housing) is a major in-kind benefit channel the held stack touches only via SAFMR rent caps and CHAS housing-problem shares — never via *who actually receives the subsidy*. Picture gives the **geographic intensity** of assisted housing (tract/county/ZIP — joins our county context warehouse directly), and PUMS gives the **tenant-level** household characteristics. Section 214 makes citizenship a live eligibility variable (mixed-status families get prorated subsidies), so this is a genuine immigration-fiscal channel with a policy hook (CRS R46462).
**Limit / honesty:** **the high-value, scriptable Picture product has NO citizenship field (verified) — this is an AHS/HCUP-class flag.** Citizenship is reachable only via (a) HUD Tenant PUMS (confirm the citizenship variable's presence + disclosure-masking in the PUMS dictionary before relying — eligible-noncitizen status may be suppressed or coarse) or (b) an ACS↔HUD linkage (the open-source `hudlink` tool, MIT, does exactly this: derives program eligibility + protected-class flags by linking IPUMS-ACS to Picture — but that is *eligibility imputation*, not an administrative citizenship count). Picture = aggregate; PUMS = sample. Subsidy receipt ≠ cost-to-government per household without the payment-standard data. [SOURCE: huduser.gov/portal/datasets/pictures/dictionary_2024.pdf]
**Graph fit:** `housing_assistance` layer; Picture joins by `state_fips`/`county_fips`/tract/ZIP (geographic intensity, NO nativity — ecological, like our SAFMR layer); HUD-PUMS joins tenant household × program (citizenship only if the PUMS variable survives disclosure); pairs with SAFMR/CHAS housing layers and the `hudlink` ACS-linkage tool for eligibility modeling.

### WIC-PPC — USDA FNS WIC Participant & Program Characteristics (migrant-farmworker flag; citizenship LIKELY ABSENT)
**Source:** USDA Food & Nutrition Service, Office of Policy Support (contractor: Westat/Insight/Abt) · **Access:** PUBLIC — biennial report PDF + downloadable aggregate datasets (Ag Data Commons; 2018 was the most recent downloadable file per the WIC Hub note), no registration; report PDFs scriptable, individual-level subset by request to FNS.
**Official:** data-visualization hub https://www.fns.usda.gov/data-research/data-visualization/wic-participant-program-characteristics · WIC PC 2022 report (PDF, Feb 2024) https://fns-prod.azureedge.us/sites/default/files/resource-files/wic-ppc-2022-report.pdf · PC 2020 landing https://www.fns.usda.gov/research/wic/ppc/2020 · data.gov catalog (2018) https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/wic-participant-and-program-characteristics-2018 · WIC Hub dataset note (access/microdata caveats) https://thewichub.org/wic-participant-and-program-characteristics/ [SOURCE: fns.usda.gov/data-research/data-visualization/wic-participant-program-characteristics]
**Format / size:** biennial (even years) since 1988; **census of all active WIC certifications** in April of the reference year, from all 89 WIC state agencies (50 states + DC + 5 territories + 33 ITOs). ~6.8 M participants (April 2022). Report = aggregate tables; a smaller individual-level subset is available by request.
**Key variables — DOES IT HAVE CITIZENSHIP? LIKELY NO; it HAS a migrant-farmworker flag.** Confirmed variables: participant category (pregnant/postpartum/breastfeeding women, infants, children <5), **household income as % of Federal Poverty Guideline** (57% <100% FPG in 2022 — the project's low-income channel), assigned **nutritional risk**, breastfeeding initiation/duration, anthropometric + hematological (hemoglobin) measures, race/ethnicity, **and — confirmed in the 2016/2018 report descriptions — "WIC members of MIGRANT farm-worker families"** (a migrant-status flag, NOT a citizenship field). The report descriptions and key-findings do NOT surface a citizenship/nativity variable.
**Why it fits:** WIC is a major in-kind nutrition-benefit channel for low-income mothers and young children — directly adjacent to the birthright-citizen-child fiscal channel (most WIC infant/child participants in immigrant households are US-born citizens). It is also notable as one of the few federal benefits available **regardless of immigration status** (no qualified-alien bar), making participation-vs-nativity a distinct policy question from TANF/SSI. The migrant-farmworker subgroup ties to the held NAWS/ag-labor focus.
**Limit / honesty — AHS/HCUP-class flag:** **the WIC PPC public product almost certainly carries NO citizenship/nativity field** (WIC eligibility is status-blind, so the program has little reason to collect it; the report instead flags migrant-farmworker household status). Treat citizenship as ABSENT until a codebook confirms otherwise — analysis of WIC-by-nativity needs an external bridge (CPS/ACS WIC-receipt-by-nativity, which the held CPS-FSS/SIPP layers already provide better). The high-value downloadable file lags (2018 last full public subset per the WIC Hub); recent years are report + aggregate visualizations only. April-snapshot census, not a panel. [SOURCE: thewichub.org/wic-participant-and-program-characteristics] [UNVERIFIED — citizenship-field presence; default ABSENT]
**Graph fit:** `welfare_admin` / nutrition layer; participant-category × income-FPG × state × biennial-year, PLUS a migrant-farmworker flag; **no nativity join on the WIC product itself** (ecological, or use CPS-FSS for WIC-by-nativity); pairs with stage5 SNAP and the TANF child tables for the citizen-child-benefit channel.

## Axis 2 — Fertility / birthright (the birthright-citizenship fiscal channel)

### NVSS-NATALITY — NCHS/NVSS Natality (births) by MATERNAL NATIVITY — VERIFIED present
**Source:** CDC National Center for Health Statistics, Division of Vital Statistics (National Vital Statistics System) · **Access:** PUBLIC, two tiers — (a) **CDC WONDER** online Natality databases (query + extract, free, scriptable via the WONDER request API); (b) **public-use micro-data files** via Vital Statistics Online (zip downloads, free, Data Users Agreement click-through). Fine geography (county <100k pop) + finest birthplace detail are RESTRICTED via the NCHS RDC.
**Official:** Natality Information hub https://wonder.cdc.gov/natality.html · WONDER **2016-2024 expanded** documentation (lists "Mother's Nativity" + "Mother's Birth Country" as groupable variables) https://wonder.cdc.gov/wonder/help/natality-expanded.html · 1995-2024 docs https://wonder.cdc.gov/wonder/help/natality.html · public-use micro-data download portal https://cdc.gov/nchs/data_access/Vitalstatsonline.htm · 2023 PUF User Guide https://ftp.cdc.gov/pub/Health_Statistics/NCHS/Dataset_Documentation/DVS/natality/UserGuide2023.pdf · restricted-data policy https://cdc.gov/nchs/nvss/nvss-restricted-data.htm [SOURCE: wonder.cdc.gov/wonder/help/natality-expanded.html]
**Format / size:** **ALL registered US live births** (not a sample) — ~3.6 M births/yr; micro-data zip per year (~few hundred MB), WONDER covers 1995–2024 (expanded items 2016+). 57 vital-statistics jurisdictions.
**Key variables — DOES IT HAVE MATERNAL NATIVITY? YES — VERIFIED.** CDC WONDER Natality (2016-2024 expanded) exposes **"Mother's Nativity" (Born in the U.S. / Born outside the U.S.)** AND **"Mother's Birth Country"** as selectable group-by dimensions (confirmed via primary documentation; `verify_claim` confidence 1 — this OVERTURNED an initial inference that nativity was restricted: the Natality file DIFFERS from the Mortality/Multiple-Cause file, which has NO nativity per the held batch4 finding). Plus: mother's age, race/Hispanic-origin, education, marital status, state + county of residence, live-birth order/parity, prenatal care, **payment source for delivery (Medicaid vs private — a direct cost signal)**, birth weight, plurality. The birth certificate's "Mother's Place of Birth" item is the source.
**Why it fits — THE birthright-citizenship fiscal channel, directly.** Every US birth is a US citizen by jus soli; births to foreign-born mothers are the precise quantity behind the birthright-citizenship debate and the downstream public-cost stream (the citizen child's lifetime eligibility for benefits, schooling). This is the ONE dataset that counts those births nationally, by state/county, with **payment-source (Medicaid)** on the same record — so "births to foreign-born mothers paid by Medicaid" (a heavily-cited cost figure) is computable directly rather than estimated. Mother's-birth-country joins the origin layer. Total enumeration → no sampling error even for fine cells (subject to suppression).
**Limit / honesty:** "Mother's Nativity" = foreign-born vs US-born; it is **NOT legal status** (foreign-born mothers include citizens, LPRs, and the unauthorized indistinguishably). Mother's-birth-country is collapsed on WONDER/public-file; finest country + sub-county geography are RDC-restricted. WONDER suppresses cells of 1-9 births. Births are an annual flow, not a population. The Medicaid "payment source" is the expected payer at delivery, not a verified cost. Confirm the exact nativity variable name + granularity on the specific PUF year before relying on the micro-data (WONDER is the verified surface). [SOURCE: cdc.gov/nchs/nvss/nvss-restricted-data.htm]
**Graph fit:** `fertility` / birthright layer (new); `mother_nativity` × `mother_birth_country` × `state_fips`/`county_fips` × payment-source × year; mother-birth-country joins origin (`POBP`); state/county joins context warehouse; the births-to-foreign-born numerator for any birthright-cost estimate, pairs with the TANF child + SSI tables (citizen-child benefit) and the held MEPS/Emergency-Medicaid layers (maternity/infant cost).

## Axis 3 — Substance / deviance (substance use + criminal-behavior self-report)

### NSDUH-NATIVITY — National Survey on Drug Use & Health (substance + self-report crime; nativity is RESTRICTED-FILE ONLY)
**Source:** SAMHSA, Center for Behavioral Health Statistics & Quality (CBHSQ); fielded by RTI International · **Access:** TWO tiers — (a) **public-use file (PUF)**, free, fully scriptable (SAS/SPSS/Stata/ASCII/R + setup files), annual 1979→ ; (b) **restricted-use Final Analytic File** via the SAMHSA/interagency Standard Application Process (SAP) → CDC/SAMHSA RDC enclave. Also an online Data Analysis System (DAS).
**Official:** PUF download hub https://www.samhsa.gov/data/data-we-collect/nsduh-national-survey-drug-use-and-health/datafiles · 2022 PUF codebook https://www.samhsa.gov/data/system/files/media-puf-file/NSDUH-2022-DS0001-info-codebook_v2.pdf · public-vs-restricted explainer (immigrant status excluded from PUF) https://www.samhsa.gov/data/get-help/public-vs-restricted-use/what-difference-between-nsduh-public-and-restricted-use-data · ICPSR series https://www.icpsr.umich.edu/web/ICPSR/series/64 · CDC RDC NSDUH page https://www.cdc.gov/rdc/other-restricted-data/nsduh.html [SOURCE: samhsa.gov/data/get-help/public-vs-restricted-use/what-difference-between-nsduh-public-and-restricted-use-data]
**Format / size:** annual cross-section, civilian noninstitutionalized population 12+, ~67,000 interviews/yr (recent); PUF per year + combined-year PUFs (2002-2018 etc., though the 2021 web-mode redesign breaks pre-2021 pooling). Restricted Final Analytic File has the full variable set.
**Key variables — DOES IT HAVE NATIVITY? NOT ON THE PUF (VERIFIED) — restricted-file only.** `verify_claim` (confidence 1) + the SAMHSA public-vs-restricted explainer confirm the **PUF EXCLUDES immigrant-status variables (citizenship + nativity) for confidentiality** (alongside the documented stripping of most geographic variables); these exist only on the **restricted Final Analytic File** via RDC. The PUF DOES carry the substance-use core (lifetime/annual/past-month alcohol, marijuana, cocaine, heroin, hallucinogens, inhalants, Rx misuse), DSM substance-use-disorder + mental-illness measures, treatment, income, and — per the ICPSR series description — **"illegal activities and arrest record"**; confirm those arrest/criminal-behavior variables survive on the recent PUF, since `verify_claim` could not confirm their PUF presence and SAMHSA has pruned sensitive items over time.
**Why it fits:** the canonical US source on substance use + self-reported criminal behavior — the substance/deviance angle the corpus lacks entirely. The immigration-relevant finding (foreign-born have LOWER substance-use and offending rates) is exactly the disconfirmation-friendly test the project values. BUT it is honestly an RDC-tier dataset for THIS purpose.
**Limit / honesty — the strongest flag in this batch:** **the scriptable PUF has NO nativity/citizenship (verified) — the immigration split requires the restricted Final Analytic File (SAP application → RDC enclave), a real access barrier (US-only, project review, no free download).** The PUF is usable for nativity-by-substance ONLY via SAMHSA's own published immigrant-status tables (if any), not by re-analysis. Self-report under-counts stigmatized behavior; the universe EXCLUDES the institutionalized (prisons) — exactly the population a crime analysis cares about, a structural undercount. Arrest items may be pruned on recent PUFs (verify). 2021 web redesign breaks the long trend. [SOURCE: cdc.gov/rdc/other-restricted-data/nsduh.html]
**Graph fit:** `substance` / deviance layer; nativity (RESTRICTED file only) × substance-use × self-report-offending × year; no clean geography on PUF; national; the substance/deviance complement to the offender-side crime stack — flagged restricted-access for the nativity dimension, so lower build-priority than the welfare/fertility cards above.

## Niche crime — assessed, NOT carded (honest skip, per the saturation test)

These were the parent-suggested niche-crime candidates. Assessed and **deliberately skipped** — each is redundant with the held/proposed stack or fails the nativity bar:

- **National Gang Center (NGC) / National Youth Gang Survey** — `nationalgangcenter.ojp.gov`. The NYGS (law-enforcement survey of gang presence/counts, ~1996-2012) is **DISCONTINUED** and its analysis "Demographics" tab reports gang-member race/ethnicity but **NO nativity/citizenship and NO immigrant-vs-native gang-membership measure**. Gang membership by immigration status is already covered better by the held **ICPSR-39107-AZ** (self-report gang membership × immigration status, Maricopa). NGC adds no nativity dimension → SKIP.
- **BJS Recidivism (2018 update; 24-state 2008 release; 34-state 2012 cohort)** — `bjs.ojp.gov`. The flagship BJS recidivism studies track rearrest/reconviction of released state prisoners but the public products report by sex/race/age/offense, **not citizenship/nativity** (citizenship is on the source NCRP/SPI records, not the recidivism tabulations). Offender-side recidivism by citizenship is already reachable via the proposed **BJS-NCRP-39234** (corrections flow with a citizenship indicator on many state submissions) + **BJS-SPI** stock. A standalone BJS-recidivism card adds no citizenship the stack lacks → SKIP.
- **NURP / National UnDACAmented Research Project (Wong/Gonzales DACA-recipient surveys)** — DACA-recipient outcome surveys (employment, wages, education gains post-DACA) DO have rich outcomes the USCIS active-recipient COUNTS lack, BUT they are **non-probability advocacy-affiliated samples** (recruited via immigrant-rights networks, no sampling frame) → strong selection bias, not a population dataset; the rigorous DACA-fiscal evidence is already on the books via **USCIS active-DACA counts** (population stock) + the **NIS/CILS/Add-Health** longitudinal panels for outcomes. A NURP card would be an advocacy-survey caveat-heavy addition with no population validity → SKIP (note its existence, don't card).
