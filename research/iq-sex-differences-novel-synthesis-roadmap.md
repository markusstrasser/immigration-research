## 1. Causal-Check Summary

**Observation:** the repo now repeatedly finds that sex differences do not line up on one cognitive surface. Instead, the signs and magnitudes sort into recurring families: evaluation/GPA, school-knowledge, applied/reasoning, track/quantity, process/timing, and adult accumulation. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-current-position.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-school-wedge-extended-synthesis.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-pisa2018-dif-theta-logit.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-pisa2018-time-dif-theta.md`]

**Null:** the field already knows isolated pieces of this story, so the repo is only producing another descriptive pile of cohort-specific coefficients. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-frontier-literature-audit.md`]

**Residual after null:** the repo does appear to have a stronger cross-surface representation than most single-paper literatures use, but it is still a synthesis claim until it is validated by either a richer latent-variable design, a restricted transcript/process dataset, or an original randomized experiment. [INFERENCE]

**Geometry:** repeated convergence across childhood, school, transition, adult, and item-level psychometric surfaces, with diminishing returns from additional public-use regressions. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-current-position.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-next-level-research-plan.md`]

**Magnitude:** enough replication to harden the surface-family split operationally, but not enough to claim a solved root-cause model of sex differences in `g`. [INFERENCE]

**Lag window:** early school through adulthood. The active mechanisms are not all contemporaneous. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-decisive-causal-tree.md`]

**Most likely cause (0.68):** the project’s most defensible contribution is a better representation of the problem, not a new universal sex-gap estimate. [INFERENCE]

**Top alternative (0.22):** the repo is mostly a strong but conventional synthesis, and the real novelty only appears if one of the restricted-data or experimental designs lands. [INFERENCE]

**Falsifier:** a literature or data program showing that these apparent surface families collapse once batteries are put onto the same latent scale and transcript/process descendants are handled cleanly. [INFERENCE]

**Decision impact:** the next work should be designed as a small number of paper-shaped contributions with explicit estimands, not as another open-ended trawl through public datasets. [INFERENCE]

## 2. Better Claims

These are the sharper claims the repo should prefer over looser slogans.

### A. Replace "the sex gap in intelligence" with "a surface-family decomposition"

**Better claim:** the project does not support one stable sex gap object. It supports a recurring decomposition into partially separable surface families:

1. `evaluation` — grades, GPA, recommendations, recognition
2. `school-knowledge` — `Math Knowledge`, broad curriculum-linked math, label-sensitive school surfaces
3. `applied/reasoning` — applied problems, arithmetic reasoning, advanced reasoning-heavy math
4. `track/quantity` — course ladders, credits, advanced participation
5. `process/timing` — speed, visits, completion, response-time structure
6. `adult accumulation` — numeracy and later use-dependent surfaces

[SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-school-wedge-extended-synthesis.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-timss-cognitive-split.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-pisa2018-time-dif-theta.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-piaac-frontier.md`]

### B. Replace "girls have a quantitative edge in NLSY97" with "the female late-school anomaly localizes to school-knowledge-heavy surfaces"

**Better claim:** the `NLSY97` anomaly is not a broad female quantitative edge. It is a localized school-knowledge-heavy wedge concentrated in `Math Knowledge` and aligned school-linked surfaces, while `PIAT Math` and `Arithmetic Reasoning` do not move with it. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-nlsy97-piat-cat-pass.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-nlsy97-transcript-deep-pass.md`]

### C. Replace "teacher bias explains the wedge" with "evaluation-family surfaces are distinct from tested-math surfaces"

**Better claim:** the late-school female-leaning family is most stable on evaluation-like surfaces, not on transcript quantity or tested math. That is a stronger and cleaner claim than saying teachers are simply biased. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-school-wedge-synthesis.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-school-wedge-mechanism-triage.md`]

### D. Replace "timing explains the school-age gap" with "timing is broad, score residuals are localized"

**Better claim:** broad female-slower timing exists on `PISA`, but it does not align tightly enough with the localized male score-family geometry to be the whole explanation. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-pisa2018-time-dif-theta.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-pisa2018-dif-theta-logit.md`]

### E. Replace "early-school data are contradictory" with "the directional child branch is mostly closed on a relative bridge"

**Better claim:** once child math is anchored to reading/verbal surfaces and age is aligned, the public child branch points in the same male direction on relative `math versus verbal` geometry. What remains open is magnitude, psychometrics, and absolute-scale comparability. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-early-school-age-matched-alignment.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-child-bridge-multi-anchor.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-public-child-branch-ach.md`]

### F. Replace "males have higher general ability" with a narrower matrix claim

**Better claim:** males appear modestly better on some figural-spatial / matrix-like surfaces, but that is not equivalent to a solved battery-independent `g` claim. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-raven-open-data.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-matrix-certainty-plan.md`]

## 3. What Looks Novel Enough

### 3.1 Surface-family synthesis

This is the strongest candidate.

The literature already knows:

1. grades and tests differ
2. timing can matter
3. advanced-track math differs from broad school math
4. some early-school divergence emerges quickly

[SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-frontier-literature-audit.md`; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11521978/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10311959/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11607718/; https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-025-09126-4]

What the repo adds is a more general representation:

> the disagreement across the literature is not random measurement noise; it is structured by recurring surface families with different causal parents.

[INFERENCE]

This is not a new raw fact. It is a more useful causal map.

### 3.2 The localized late-school wedge

The narrowing of the `NLSY97` anomaly from "female quantitative advantage" to "school-knowledge-heavy wedge" is stronger than casual discourse in the field. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-nlsy97-piat-cat-pass.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-nlsy97-transcript-deep-pass.md`]

This could be publishable if it is replicated in a richer transcript-linked cohort. [INFERENCE]

### 3.3 Timing-score decoupling

The repo’s `PISA` stack now supports a specific statement:

1. broad female-slower timing is real
2. localized male score-residual families are real
3. the second does not reduce to the first

[SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-pisa2018-time-dif-theta.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-pisa2018-dif-theta-logit.md`]

This is stronger than simply saying "timing matters." [INFERENCE]

### 3.4 Relative child-math stability after bridge alignment

The repo now has a multi-cohort child result:

1. raw child surfaces disagree
2. aligned relative surfaces converge directionally
3. teacher behavior/evaluation does not reverse that direction

[SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-early-school-age-matched-alignment.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-psid-cds-teacher-pass.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-public-child-branch-ach.md`]

That is not fully novel as a developmental claim, but it is a nontrivial resolution of an apparent internal contradiction. [INFERENCE]

## 4. What Is Not Novel Enough

Do not pitch these as the main contribution:

1. "measurement matters" — already well known [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-frontier-literature-audit.md`]
2. "grades and tests differ" — already well known [SOURCE: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11521978/]
3. "boys do better on some spatial/math surfaces" — already well known [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-verification.md`]

The novelty has to come from integration, validation, or manipulation. [INFERENCE]

## 5. Best New Research Directions

## 5.1 A Multitrait-Multimethod Surface Model

**Best idea:** stop treating every battery as a new trait. Treat many of them as different methods measuring overlapping latent families.

### Core move

Build a cross-dataset multitrait-multimethod representation:

- traits:
  - `relative verbal`
  - `school-knowledge math`
  - `applied/reasoning math`
  - `visuospatial/figural`
  - `evaluation/performance`
- methods:
  - standardized test
  - transcript/grade
  - recommendation/recognition
  - process/timing
  - adult outcome

This is methodologically legitimate and underused in this literature. [SOURCE: https://www.cambridge.org/core/product/02A7E7AD369AD0DD50FF1A5AD449ACD9/core-reader; https://www.researchgate.net/publication/248780626_Analyzing_Multitrait-Multimethod_Data_A_Comparison_of_Three_Approaches]

### Why this matters

It directly tests the repo’s core synthesis claim:

> are the sex differences mostly between traits, or between methods applied to similar traits?

[INFERENCE]

### Publishable output

Paper-shaped claim:

> sex differences in cognitive/academic performance are better represented as multitrait-multimethod surface families than as one latent gap or many unrelated anomalies.

## 5.2 Transcript IRT / Course-Strength Modeling

This is a concrete upgrade for the school pipeline branch.

Recent work has already shown that transcript strength can be estimated with IRT by treating courses as items and grades as ordered responses. [SOURCE: https://files.eric.ed.gov/fulltext/ED671123.pdf]

### Why this matters here

Right now the repo separates:

1. transcript quantity
2. grades/GPA
3. tested math

But transcript quantity is crude. Transcript IRT would let us estimate:

- course difficulty
- transcript strength
- transcript strength conditional on course selection

Then compare sex gaps on:

1. transcript theta
2. tested-math theta
3. GPA/evaluation residuals

This would be much better than raw credits or ladder dummies. [INFERENCE]

## 5.3 Measurement Invariance *and* Prediction Invariance

This is the cleanest conceptual upgrade.

The literature often checks one but not both. Millsap explicitly notes how rare it is to jointly examine measurement and prediction invariance in the same data. [SOURCE: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11336-018-9649-2]

### Proposed matrix

For any surface, evaluate:

1. measurement invariance by sex
2. prediction invariance by sex

That yields a four-cell classification:

1. invariant measure, invariant prediction
2. invariant measure, non-invariant prediction
3. non-invariant measure, invariant prediction
4. non-invariant measure, non-invariant prediction

### Why this matters

This gives the project a clearer answer to "so what?"

It distinguishes:

- fair latent measures
- useful but method-loaded screens
- unfair or unstable measures
- school-performance indicators that are predictive even if not trait-pure

[INFERENCE]

## 5.4 Process-Based DIF Reduction Instead of More DIF Detection

The repo already has enough DIF detection.

The next useful step is reduction or reinterpretation using process data, which the frontier is now starting to do. [SOURCE: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11607718/; https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s40536-026-00290-1; https://www.cambridge.org/core/services/aop-cambridge-core/content/view/69A15CC6FFB38BDCB1F9C0FBDE550A6D/S0033312325100720a.pdf/div-class-title-reducing-differential-item-functioning-via-process-data-div.pdf]

### Better question

Not:

> do items show DIF?

But:

> how much residual DIF disappears if process nuisance is modeled explicitly?

That would convert the current `PISA` branch from repeated hardening to an actual mechanism test. [INFERENCE]

## 5.5 An Original Randomized Matrix Experiment

This is the clearest way to stop arguing from observational surfaces alone.

Use the local `OMIB` item bank and randomize:

1. tight vs generous time limit
2. school-framed vs neutral framing
3. diagram salience / perceptual clutter
4. answer format or interface burden where feasible

[SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-matrix-experiment-protocol.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-open-matrix-assets.md`]

### Why this matters

If the male matrix edge changes materially under manipulated time/format/frame, then the repo gains direct causal evidence that matrix gaps are at least partly surface-made rather than just latent-domain differences. [INFERENCE]

## 5.6 A Blinded Grading / Recommendation Audit

The late-school evaluation family is stable enough that the next honest test is a randomized evaluation study.

Recent relevant designs exist:

- AP recommendation audit [SOURCE: https://ideas.repec.org/a/aea/apandp/v114y2024p205-09.html]
- factorial teacher-status experiment [SOURCE: https://sociologicalscience.com/articles-v11-27-743/]
- teacher stereotype video experiment [SOURCE: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/397308627_Teachers'_awareness_of_math-gender_stereotype-reinforcing_behaviors_in_the_classroom_a_video_experiment]

### Better question

Not:

> are teachers biased?

But:

> how much of the evaluation-family wedge can be recreated experimentally with fixed student work and randomized student identity/performance context?

[INFERENCE]

## 6. Paper-Shaped Outputs

## Paper 1. The Surface Families Paper

**Claim:** sex differences in cognitive and academic performance are better represented as cross-surface families than as one stable gap.

**Current support:** already substantial. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-school-wedge-extended-synthesis.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-current-position.md`]

**What upgrades it:** a formal MTMM / latent-family model.

## Paper 2. The Localized Late-School Wedge Paper

**Claim:** the most replicable female-leaning late-school branch is evaluation and school-knowledge, not broad tested math, transcript quantity, or applied reasoning.

**Current support:** `NLSY97`, `HSLS`, `ELS`, `NELS`, `Add Health`, `PSID TAS`. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-school-wedge-extended-synthesis.md`]

**What upgrades it:** `AHAA` or other restricted transcript access.

## Paper 3. The PISA Measurement Paper

**Claim:** residual male-leaning item families survive increasingly strong public psychometric stress tests, while broad female-slower timing does not map cleanly onto them.

**Current support:** strong local stack. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-pisa2018-dif-theta-logit.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-pisa2018-time-dif-theta.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-pisa2018-dif-2pl-anchor.md`]

**What upgrades it:** process-based DIF reduction or a better joint latent-variable toolchain.

## Paper 4. The Matrix Experiment Paper

**Claim:** some matrix-like sex differences are design-sensitive rather than fixed latent-domain gaps.

**Current support:** protocol and item bank are ready, but no randomized data yet. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-matrix-experiment-protocol.md`]

**What upgrades it:** actual experimental data.

## 7. What Not To Do

1. Do not write a paper whose main thesis is simply "the sex gap in IQ is complicated."
2. Do not lead with `g`. The repo is stronger on surface families than on global latent adjudication.
3. Do not keep adding public-use cohorts unless they identify a new mechanism or a new method class.
4. Do not use descendant-heavy attenuation tables as if they were mediation.
5. Do not claim novelty for isolated facts the recent literature already knows.

---

## 8. Recommended Order

1. **Elevate the synthesis now**
   - make the surface-family representation the canonical top-line claim
2. **File the restricted Add Health/AHAA request**
   - best live data upgrade
3. **Prototype one formal MTMM / prediction-invariance analysis**
   - even a small pilot on one cohort pair is enough to test the representation
4. **Run one original experiment**
   - `OMIB` timing experiment is the fastest path to direct causal evidence
5. **Only then revisit stronger `g` claims**
   - after the methods and pipeline surfaces are better separated

## 9. Best Current Answer

The repo’s best contribution is not a new universal sex-gap number.

It is a sharper statement:

> what looks like one argument about sex and intelligence is actually an entangled set of method families, school-pipeline families, and developmental stages. The stable object is not "the IQ gap." The stable object is the geometry of how different surfaces diverge and recombine.

[INFERENCE]

That is publishable only if it is pushed one step further by:

1. a richer latent-variable design,
2. a restricted transcript/process dataset, or
3. a randomized manipulation.

Without one of those, it remains a strong synthesis. With one of those, it becomes stronger research.
