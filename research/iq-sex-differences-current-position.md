## Evidence Recital

1. The literature does **not** establish a single stable, battery-independent sex difference in general intelligence `g`; domain differences are more stable than overall composite differences. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-verification.md`]
2. In `NLSY79`, the same-sample schooling pass only partly attenuates female verbal / clerical edges and does not collapse male quantitative / mechanical patterns. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-second-pass-results.md`]
3. In `PIAAC`, adult numeracy is male-leaning across country-waves, education groups, age bands, and broad occupation groups in the current local snapshot. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-piaac-frontier.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-piaac-age-occupation.md`]
4. In `TIMSS`, broad school math shifts femaleward from Grade 4 to Grade 8 in the overlap countries, while `TIMSS Advanced` upper-track mathematics remains male-leaning. That makes "math gap" an explicitly multi-surface object in the project. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-timss-frontier.md`]
5. In `NLSY97`, the raw quantitative female edge is fragile; on the larger `process_core` sample it moves from same-sample base `+0.037` to adjusted `-0.041`. That is a real robustness warning, but the current Stage A block should be read as a stress test rather than clean mechanism identification because it mixes nuisance, mechanism, and descendant variables. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-nlsy97-stage-a.md`; `research/review-1-audit.md`]
6. The project has already exposed one concrete data-handling risk: the first frozen `NLSY97` sex field was wrong for the local public extract and had to be corrected. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-second-pass-results.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-analysis-protocol.md`]
7. In the first transcript-rich `HSLS` pass, girls are near parity at baseline math and slightly below boys on the later standardized math surface, but clearly above boys on transcript math GPA, and that wedge persists within common highest-math ladders. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-hsls-wedge-first-pass.md`]
8. In the new same-cohort `NLSY97` `PIAT`/`CAT` pass, the female-looking quantitative signal does not generalize across youth math surfaces: `PIAT Math` is slightly male-leaning / near null in the overlap sample, `Arithmetic Reasoning` is slightly male-leaning / near null, and the female signal is concentrated in `Math Knowledge`. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-nlsy97-piat-cat-pass.md`]
9. In the focused `TIMSS` cognitive split, broad school-math surfaces move femaleward from Grade 4 to Grade 8 most strongly on `knowing`, while `TIMSS Advanced` remains male-leaning across all domains and especially on `reasoning`. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-timss-cognitive-split.md`]
10. In the first `PISA 2018` item/process pass, broad math is male-leaning, `43/60` math items are male-leaning, and the female-positive items do not cluster cleanly by simple burden quartile or generic time/visit load. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-pisa2018-item-split.md`]
11. The newest frontier literature suggests some male-favoring broad school-math divergence can emerge very early after school entry, which means late-school transcript and advanced-track mechanisms are not sufficient as a full causal story. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-frontier-literature-audit.md`]
12. In the `PISA 2018` framework-proxy pass, the item heterogeneity is now visibly structured: proxy `space_shape` is the most male-leaning content family, `change_relationships` is next, and proxy `uncertainty_data` is closest to parity. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-pisa2018-framework-proxy.md`]
13. In the first local `NLSCYA` early-school pass, `PIAT Math` standard scores are female-leaning at younger ages and move toward parity by later childhood, so the local early-school node does not yet support a simple immediate male-edge story. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-nlscya-early-school-first-pass.md`]
14. In the first `ECLS-K:2011` early-school replication, math is near parity in kindergarten and then becomes male-leaning by spring first and second grade, while reading stays female-leaning throughout. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-eclsk2011-early-school-first-pass.md`]
15. In the older `ECLS-K` school-entry cohort, broad math is already modestly male-leaning in kindergarten and becomes more male-leaning by spring first grade, while reading stays female-leaning throughout. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-eclsk-early-school-first-pass.md`]
16. In the first early-school score-alignment pass, the raw `NLSCYA` female-looking math edge mostly collapses once math is anchored to same-wave reading. All three local child cohorts then show male-leaning `math minus reading` residuals. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-early-school-score-alignment.md`]
17. In the age-matched early-school bridge, `NLSCYA` and `ECLS-K:2011` remain directionally aligned across overlapping `84` to `126` month windows: both show male-leaning `math minus reading` residuals, with `ECLS-K:2011` more male-leaning in magnitude. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-early-school-age-matched-alignment.md`]
18. In the new public item face-validity audit, released `PISA` items and official `ASVAB` sample items do not show an obvious overt sex-coded “smoking gun,” but they do show clear construct-loading differences between spatial-layout, graph/table, and formula-schooling burdens. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-item-face-validity-audit.md`]
19. In the new contamination-aware `PISA 2018` leave-out pass, the broad raw male item lean still shrinks under conditioning, but the residual family ordering survives focal-item and focal-family exclusion: `space_shape` remains the strongest male-residual family, `change_relationships` remains next, and `uncertainty_data` remains closest to parity. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-pisa2018-dif-leaveout.md`]
20. In the first deep `NLSY97` transcript pass, the late-school wedge survives `PIAT` anchoring plus design, self-reported exposure, and school-offer controls: `Math Knowledge` stays female-leaning, `Arithmetic Reasoning` does not, and transcript math GPA / credits remain female-leaning. The only clean collapse is in an evaluation-heavy block that keeps just `125` to `239` cases and is therefore too attrited to settle the mechanism. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-nlsy97-transcript-deep-pass.md`]
21. In the first DAG-valid `NLSY97` course-exposure pass, current advanced-course flags do not provide a clean explanation for the `Math Knowledge` wedge. Under the observed-confounder set validated for `PrecalcPlus1997 -> MathKnowledge`, the exposure coefficients are small to negative, the female `Math Knowledge` residual remains positive, and robustness values are low (`RV = 0.0377` for `Math Knowledge`, `0.0163` for `Arithmetic Reasoning`). [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-nlsy97-course-dag-robustness.md`]
22. In the first `NLSY97` pre-test behavior/compliance versus climate/fairness pass, the available 1997 behavior and school-climate surfaces do not materially explain the late-school wedge: they show weak sex separation, barely move the female residual on `Math Knowledge` or transcript math GPA, transcript-observability IPW barely changes the results, and the one non-null `behavior -> MathKnowledge` estimate is sample-sensitive and fragile. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-nlsy97-behavior-evaluation-pass.md`]
23. In the first public-use `ELS` replication, later tested math is male-leaning, while school-linked track and evaluation surfaces tilt female: girls are more likely to reach `algebra II+` and `trig/precalc/calc`, more likely to be recognized for good grades, and slightly more likely to receive a math-teacher `AP/honors` recommendation once baseline achievement is held fixed. At the same time, the later standardized math surface remains male-leaning, and the observed `ELS` behavior/compliance block does not explain that split. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-els-wedge-first-pass.md`]
24. In the first `HSLS` refinement, adding the obvious pre-follow-up homework, self-efficacy, belonging, teacher-encouragement, and math-teacher climate surfaces does not materially compress the transcript GPA or `precalc_plus` wedge. The later standardized-math surface becomes somewhat less male-leaning, but the school-linked wedge survives and slightly strengthens on the common sample. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-hsls-wedge-refinement.md`]
25. In the first public-use `NELS` replication, later tested math is near parity at `F1` and male-leaning by `F2`, while school-linked evaluation surfaces tilt female: math grade quality, recognition for good grades, and academic-honors recommendation stay female-leaning after base-year anchoring, but transcript math units are near neutral. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-nels-wedge-first-pass.md`]
26. In the first cleaned public-use `PSID CDS` pass, reading surfaces are female-leaning, applied-problems math is male-leaning across `1997`, `2002`, and `2007`, and the aligned child `math minus reading` surface remains male-leaning both descriptively and in a family fixed-effects specification. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-psid-cds-first-pass.md`]
27. In the first cleaned public-use `PSID TAS` pass, normalized high-school and college GPA surfaces are female-leaning, `SAT math` and `ACT` surfaces are male-leaning, and the aligned `GPA minus SAT math` wedge remains female-leaning in a family fixed-effects specification. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-psid-tas-transition-first-pass.md`]
28. In the first canonical public-use `Add Health` pass, girls are ahead on all four wave II subject-grade surfaces, that grade advantage survives a wave I verbal-plus-family-background block, and both math and English grades strongly predict wave IV attainment under a DAG-valid observed-confounder set. The pattern is broader than math alone because English remains more female-tilted than math. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-addhealth-school-surface-first-pass.md`]
29. In the first `FFCWS` pass, the Year 9 child battery does not show a simple broad male-math edge: `Applied Problems` is near parity to slightly female-leaning, reading comprehension is clearly female-leaning, `PPVT` is slightly male-leaning, and the relative math-versus-verbal split depends on the verbal anchor. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-ffcws-achievement-first-pass.md`]
30. In that same `FFCWS` pass, Year 22 college exposure is clearly female-leaning, and the female college-years residual survives the Year 9 battery plus baseline family-background block. Year 9 applied-problems math remains one of the strongest observed predictors of later college years. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-ffcws-achievement-first-pass.md`]
31. In the first `PISA 2018` process-nuisance prototype, adding non-focal time/visit/count style to the contamination-aware leave-out match does not materially compress the residual content-family ordering. The weighted overall residual becomes slightly more male (`-0.0075 -> -0.0107`), `space_shape` stays the strongest male-residual family, and mean absolute shrinkage is effectively zero. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-pisa2018-process-nuisance.md`]
32. In the first `PISA 2018` response-time DIF pilot, girls are slower on `57/60` math items after the non-focal-family accuracy match, and every content family shows a positive weighted female time residual. But the time geometry is broad while the score-residual geometry remains localized and only weakly aligned to it. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-pisa2018-time-dif-pilot.md`]
31. In the new child multi-anchor and rank-sensitivity passes, the public child bridge is no longer reading-only: `PSID CDS` remains male-leaning under every tested verbal anchor and within-family rank-based checks, `NLSCYA` remains male under reading and mean-verbal anchors while `PPVT` alone stays weak and unstable, and `FFCWS` keeps the same anchor ordering in later-child data. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-child-bridge-multi-anchor.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-child-bridge-rank-sensitivity.md`]
32. In the new public-use outcome decomposition, the female later-attainment edge behaves very differently across life-course surfaces: in `Add Health`, adolescent grade surfaces explain a meaningful but partial share of the female wave-IV attainment edge on the common sample, while in `FFCWS`, the Year 9 cognitive battery barely attenuates the female Year-22 college-years edge. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-school-outcome-decomposition.md`]
33. In the new public-use outcome-interaction pass, `Add Health` grade predictors do not show meaningfully weaker downstream attainment slopes for girls than for boys, and `FFCWS` child-score interactions are mostly negative but imprecise, with `PPVT` the only near-nonzero case. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-school-outcome-interactions.md`]

## Current Best Take

### Established enough to use operationally

1. The measurement layer is first-order, not a nuisance footnote. Battery composition, timing, adaptivity, score construction, and subgroup-specific process variables can materially change the sign or magnitude of observed sex gaps. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-verification.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-timing-channels.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-nlsy97-stage-a.md`]
2. Domain-specific differences are more stable than any single overall `g` story. Male-favoring spatial / mechanical / adult numeracy surfaces and female-favoring clerical-speed surfaces replicate more cleanly than any battery-independent composite gap. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-verification.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-piaac-frontier.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-second-pass-results.md`]
3. School math is not a single stable surface either. `TIMSS` broad Grade 8 math and `TIMSS Advanced` upper-track math point in different directions, which raises the priority of curriculum, track, and selection nodes. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-timss-frontier.md`]
4. The strong version of the "schooling / educator attention explains most of the pattern" story is weakened. Current local evidence supports it as a partial channel for some verbal / clerical surfaces, not as a master explanation. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-second-pass-results.md`]
5. The raw `NLSY97` quantitative female edge should no longer be treated as strong evidence for a general female quantitative advantage. More narrowly: the current Stage A pass is enough to kill a confident “`NLSY97` discovered a real female quant edge” story, but not enough to identify a dominant causal mechanism. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-nlsy97-stage-a.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-next-node-validation.md`; `research/review-1-audit.md`]
6. The school-linked evaluation / placement node is now materially stronger than it was before the raw NCES work. The current `HSLS` footprint says grades and some placement surfaces are not just noisy versions of tested math, though the within-ladder comparisons should still be read as descriptive rather than causally clean. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-hsls-wedge-first-pass.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-evaluation-placement-node.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-model-review-2026-03-06.md`]
7. The current `NLSY97` anomaly is narrower than “female quantitative advantage.” The best-supported local description is a school-knowledge / `Math Knowledge`-heavy wedge, not a broad same-cohort female edge across all available youth math surfaces. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-nlsy97-piat-cat-pass.md`]
8. The `TIMSS` cognitive split is directionally consistent with that narrower description: `knowing` moves femaleward faster than `applying` from Grade 4 to Grade 8, but advanced-track mathematics remains male-leaning and especially male on `reasoning`. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-timss-cognitive-split.md`]
9. The first `PISA 2018` pass weakens any simple “generic process burden explains the school-age split” story. The broad `PISA` math surface is male-leaning, and the item-level heterogeneity does not sort cleanly by burden quartile alone. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-pisa2018-item-split.md`]
10. The first `PISA 2018` framework-proxy pass says the remaining school-age heterogeneity is not random: the strongest male-leaning block is the proxy `space_shape` family, while proxy `uncertainty_data` is closest to parity. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-pisa2018-framework-proxy.md`]
11. The causal tree now needs an explicit early-school emergence node, but the old “child cohorts point in opposite directions” summary is too crude. After the reading-anchor and age-matched bridge passes, the local child cohorts align directionally on a relative `math versus reading` bridge; the remaining disagreement is degree and timing, not sign on that bridged estimand. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-frontier-literature-audit.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-decisive-causal-tree.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-early-school-score-alignment.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-early-school-age-matched-alignment.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-model-review-2026-03-06.md`]
12. The new public item audit is consistent with the current measurement-surface story: what is visually salient is construct-loading, not an obvious overtly sex-coded page design. That is useful as a sanity check, but it is still weaker than real DIF or invariant-item analysis. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-item-face-validity-audit.md`]
13. The contamination-aware `PISA` leave-out pass now strengthens the school-age measurement story again: a large share of the raw school-age male lean still attenuates under conditioning, but the residual family pattern survives when the matching score excludes the focal item or focal family. That means the residual geometry is no longer just a plausible-value proxy artifact, even though it is still weaker than full formal DIF. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-pisa2018-dif-leaveout.md`]
14. The first deep `NLSY97` transcript pass hardens the same-cohort wedge as a real late-school object. `Math Knowledge` and transcript math surfaces stay female-leaning after `PIAT`, design, self-reported exposure, and school-offer controls, while `Arithmetic Reasoning` does not move with them. That weakens simple course-taking explanations and pushes the live mechanism question toward behavior / compliance and evaluation rather than generic exposure alone. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-nlsy97-transcript-deep-pass.md`]
15. The first explicit DAG audit changes the regression discipline. For the total effect of `sex` on late-school `Math Knowledge`, `PIAT`, current course ladder, grades, and transcript outputs are not admissible ordinary controls. For the narrower course-exposure question, the valid observed adjustment set is `{sex, age, PIAT proxy, school-offer proxies}`, and even that estimate is fragile. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-nlsy97-course-dag-robustness.md`]
16. The first internal `NLSY97` behavior/compliance screen weakens the strong behavior explanation for the late-school wedge. In this cohort, the available homework/absence/suspension and school-climate/fairness surfaces are not strongly sex-separated, do not materially compress the female `Math Knowledge` or transcript-GPA residuals, and do not produce a stable same-sample causal-behavior story. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-nlsy97-behavior-evaluation-pass.md`]
17. The school-linked wedge is now externally replicated across two U.S. longitudinal cohorts, but with different observables: `HSLS` shows a transcript-GPA versus tested-math split, while public-use `ELS` shows a track/evaluation versus tested-math split. That hardens the `SchoolPipeline` / evaluation-placement node without proving a single transcript-GPA mechanism. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-hsls-wedge-first-pass.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-els-wedge-first-pass.md`]
18. The first `HSLS` mechanism refinement weakens the simple “girls just do more homework / feel more belonging / report better teacher climate” objection. Those pre-follow-up surfaces do not materially compress the transcript-GPA or track wedge, so the school-linked split now survives a first plausible mechanism stress test in `HSLS` as well. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-hsls-wedge-refinement.md`]
19. The older `NELS` cohort now points the same way as `HSLS` and `ELS` on the core split: later tested math is male-leaning or near null, while school-linked evaluation surfaces tilt female. But `NELS` also narrows the wedge by showing that pure transcript math quantity is much less female-leaning than grades, recognition, or honors recommendation. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-nels-wedge-first-pass.md`]
20. The new cross-cohort late-school synthesis says the most stable replicated family is female-leaning school evaluation, not a universal female edge in tested math and not a universal female edge in raw course quantity. Across `HSLS`, `ELS`, `NELS`, and `NLSY97`, GPA / grades / recognition / recommendation surfaces are the cleanest female-tilting family, while tested math is more male or null and track / quantity surfaces are the least stable family. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-school-wedge-synthesis.md`]
21. The new late-school mechanism triage tightens that synthesis: the measured behavior / identity / climate / anchor blocks move tested-math surfaces somewhat, but they do not materially compress the evaluation family and they do not explain the `NLSY97` school-knowledge residual. In `NLSY97`, DAG-valid course-exposure effects remain weak to negative while the female `Math Knowledge` residual stays positive. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-school-wedge-mechanism-triage.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-nlsy97-course-dag-robustness.md`]
22. The new `PSID CDS` result materially strengthens the early-school branch because it adds a family-linked public child panel outside the `NLSY` / `ECLS` pair. On the relative child `math versus reading` bridge, the direction is now the same in `ECLS`, anchored `NLSCYA`, and `PSID CDS`; the remaining child uncertainty is magnitude and anchor choice, not sign. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-psid-cds-first-pass.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-early-school-age-matched-alignment.md`]
23. The new `PSID TAS` result extends the school-linked wedge into the transition branch. The current best read is no longer just “girls get higher grades while boys test higher in some school cohorts”; it is that female GPA-type outputs versus male tested-math outputs also reappear in a family-linked early-adult panel. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-psid-tas-transition-first-pass.md`]
24. The new `Add Health` result broadens that late-school picture again. Public-use `Add Health` does not give a transcript/test adjudication, but it does show that the school-performance / evaluation family is not confined to transcript-rich NCES cohorts or to `PSID` transition surfaces. The grade advantage is broad across subjects, survives a wave I verbal-plus-family-background block, and is stronger in English than math. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-addhealth-school-surface-first-pass.md`]
25. The new `FFCWS` result makes the child and transition story less slogan-friendly but more robust. In a very different cohort, the Year 9 math-versus-verbal geometry is still plural and anchor-sensitive, while the Year 22 transition wedge remains female-leaning and survives the observed Year 9 battery plus baseline family-background block. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-ffcws-achievement-first-pass.md`]
26. The public child branch is now materially more settled than it was. After the multi-anchor and rank-based passes, the early-school relative math result survives more than one anchor family and survives a family-linked public panel. `PPVT` still behaves like a weak special-case anchor, but it no longer justifies treating the whole child branch as unresolved. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-child-bridge-multi-anchor.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-child-bridge-rank-sensitivity.md`]
27. The public outcome branch now adds a real life-course split. The female attainment edge is much more sensitive to adolescent school/evaluation surfaces than to the currently staged early-childhood cognitive battery. That does not prove mediation, but it does support keeping early-childhood cognition and adolescent school/transition accumulation as separate nodes in the causal tree. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-school-outcome-decomposition.md`]
28. The first predictive-validity interaction pass weakens the lazy “female school surfaces are just inflated and low-value” story. In public `Add Health`, grades remain strongly predictive for later attainment for both sexes with no clean sex-differential payoff. `FFCWS` leaves a weaker hint that some child verbal surfaces may predict later college years more strongly for boys, but that pattern is not broad or decisive. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-school-outcome-interactions.md`]

### Not established

1. A large stable male advantage in battery-independent general ability is **not** established by the current project state. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-verification.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-piaac-frontier.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-nlsy97-stage-a.md`]
2. A strong "female-favoring pedagogy is masking a true male `g` advantage" story is **not** established either. The current footprint is too measurement-sensitive for that conclusion. [INFERENCE from `research/iq-sex-differences-second-pass-results.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-nlsy97-stage-a.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-piaac-frontier.md`]
3. "`g` is bullshit" is too strong. A weaker claim is better supported: `g` is a useful but theory-laden summary, and sex-gap claims about it are much less clean than popular discussion implies. [⚠ FRAMING-SENSITIVE] [INFERENCE from `research/iq-sex-differences-verification.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-causal-graph.md`]

## Current Causal Read

> **Observation:** the project sees stable domain asymmetries, unstable composite gaps, one fragile `NLSY97` quantitative reversal, and a broadly male-leaning adult numeracy surface. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-second-pass-results.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-nlsy97-stage-a.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-piaac-frontier.md`]
>
> **Null:** if there is no large stable sex difference in battery-independent general ability, then construct mismatch, task design, timing, adaptive scoring, schooling-linked practice, and sample-selection effects can generate the observed pattern. [INFERENCE]
>
> **Residual after null:** some specific male-favoring domains remain large and stable enough that a pure "all artifact" story is also too weak. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-second-pass-results.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-piaac-frontier.md`]

- `P(cause)`: `0.70` that the main disagreement in this project is driven more by measurement / construct / process differences than by a large stable sex difference in general ability. [INFERENCE]
- `Top alternative`: `0.20` that there is a true small male advantage on some highly `g`-loaded quantitative / spatial surfaces, but current batteries and school-exit surfaces obscure it. [INFERENCE]
- `Falsifier`: a preregistered, measurement-invariant, transcript-aware, multi-battery study showing the same male advantage across balanced batteries after process correction. [INFERENCE]
- `Decision impact`: the next work should attack the score surface and construct map before making stronger claims about latent general ability. [INFERENCE]

## Live Data-Collection And Measurement Risks

1. **Manifest / coding risk:** one concrete field-selection bug already occurred in `NLSY97`; similar issues are plausible in other public extracts. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-second-pass-results.md`]
2. **Construct mismatch risk:** `ASVAB quantitative`, `CAT-ASVAB quantitative`, `PIAAC numeracy`, `PISA math`, and `TIMSS math` are not interchangeable merely because they all sound mathematical. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-next-node-validation.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-analysis-protocol.md`]
3. **Adaptive-score risk:** completion, routing, posterior variance, and process-quality variables can matter enough to distort subgroup means. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-nlsy97-stage-a.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-next-node-validation.md`]
4. **Selection risk:** same-sample versus unrestricted estimates already moved some coefficients materially before any controls were added. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-second-pass-results.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-nlsy97-stage-a.md`]
5. **Age-surface risk:** adult numeracy, broad school math, upper-track advanced math, and school-exit adaptive quantitative tests should not be read as the same developmental surface. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-piaac-frontier.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-next-node-validation.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-timss-frontier.md`]
6. **Bad-control risk:** the first `NLSY97` Stage A process block mixes room-condition nuisance with score-generation descendants such as `items_complete` and posterior variance, so coefficient movement in that pass is not clean causal identification. [SOURCE: `research/review-1-audit.md`; `sources/iq-sex-diff/nlsy97_stage_a_pass.py`]
7. **Evaluation / placement risk:** grades, honors, recommendations, and some school-linked placement variables do contain sex-coded evaluation / classroom-performance signals in the first transcript-rich `HSLS` pass, so they should be treated as mechanism surfaces rather than clean latent-ability proxies. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-evaluation-placement-node.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-hsls-wedge-first-pass.md`]
8. **Extra-missing-code risk:** later-added transcript and `PIAT` fields in `NLSY97` use additional negative missing codes beyond the earlier Stage A surfaces, so local scripts must clean those explicitly before modeling. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-nlsy97-piat-cat-pass.md`]
9. **Early-stage omission risk:** if the project only models adolescent and adult surfaces, it can misattribute an early-emerging broad school-math divergence to later transcript, label, or advanced-track mechanisms. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-frontier-literature-audit.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-decisive-causal-tree.md`]
10. **Behavior / compliance / stakes risk:** this remains a live mechanism class, but the first `NLSY97` pre-test screen weakens the strong version of it inside that cohort. The remaining risk is narrower: the relevant engagement/stakes variables may still be under-measured locally or may matter more in external cohorts than in the available 1997 block. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-model-review-2026-03-06.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-nlsy97-behavior-evaluation-pass.md`]
11. **Relative-anchor risk:** the child bridge currently resolves the sign conflict on a relative `math versus reading` estimand, not on a battery-independent absolute math scale. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-early-school-age-matched-alignment.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-model-review-2026-03-06.md`]
12. **Non-independence risk:** repeated cuts of the same batteries, especially `PISA` and `PIAAC`, are internal stress tests rather than independent replications. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-model-review-2026-03-06.md`]
13. **Categorical-code risk:** transcript and school-program surfaces can silently fail if numeric code scales are guessed instead of checked; the first `NLSY97` `mathpipe` grouping was accidentally keyed to `1..8` rather than `100..800`. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-nlsy97-transcript-deep-pass.md`] 
14. **Estimand-slippage risk:** for the late-school anomaly, “sex effect” and “course-exposure effect” are different causal questions with different valid control sets. Mixing them produces bad-control regressions that look sophisticated but are structurally wrong. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-nlsy97-course-dag-robustness.md`]
15. **Access-gate risk:** the remaining high-leverage restricted-data frontier is now bifurcated, not generic. `Add Health` restricted-use / `AHAA` is the best live path, while `HSTS` transcript microdata and `NAEP` process data are still scientifically high-value but the official `ResearchDataGov` guide says new `NCES` `SAP` applications are paused until further notice. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-access-playbook.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-remaining-dataset-frontier.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-restricted-data-plan.md`; https://manager.researchdatagov.org/RDG_User_Guide.pdf]
16. **PSID public-file risk:** the `CDS` child panel required real public-file hygiene fixes before it became usable: `999` sentinel scores had to be recoded, `2002/2007` age fields were already in months, and `1997 broad_math` is not independent from `applied_problems` in the current public child file. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-psid-cds-first-pass.md`]
17. **PSID TAS weight risk:** the public `2019 TAS` `cross_weight` field is nonmissing but entirely zero in the current file, so weighted reuse must either fall back to the positive prior longitudinal weight or report unweighted sensitivity checks explicitly. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-psid-tas-transition-first-pass.md`]
18. **Add Health surface risk:** the public `Add Health` branch is a school-performance / later-attainment panel, not a clean tested-math panel. It sharpens the broad school-surface family but does not directly resolve transcript-versus-test or battery-independent `g`. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-addhealth-school-surface-first-pass.md`]
19. **FFCWS anchor risk:** the new `FFCWS` child geometry depends materially on which verbal anchor is used. `Applied Problems` looks male relative to reading comprehension, female relative to `PPVT`, and close to neutral relative to the average verbal anchor. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-ffcws-achievement-first-pass.md`]
20. **Add Health public-linkage risk:** the current local public `Add Health` files expose sibling/twin flags but no obvious recoverable pair identifier, so the within-family extension should be treated as blocked for now in the public snapshot. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-addhealth-public-pair-note.md`]
21. **PPVT-anchor risk:** the early-school child branch is no longer reading-only, but `PPVT` remains the least stable verbal anchor in both `NLSCYA` and `FFCWS`. Treat it as a sensitivity surface, not as the sole arbiter of child relative-math direction. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-child-bridge-multi-anchor.md`; `research/iq-sex-differences-child-bridge-rank-sensitivity.md`]
22. **Outcome-decomposition risk:** once grades, `PVT`, or early-childhood scores are added to `sex -> attainment` models, those regressions become descendant-path decompositions rather than clean total-effect estimates. Coefficient attenuation is informative, but it is not identified mediation. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-school-outcome-decomposition.md`]
23. **Outcome-interaction risk:** the new interaction pass is public-use and outcome-specific. It can weaken the simple grade-inflation story, but it is not strong enough to establish universal equality or inequality of downstream returns across all school and test surfaces. [SOURCE: `research/iq-sex-differences-school-outcome-interactions.md`]
