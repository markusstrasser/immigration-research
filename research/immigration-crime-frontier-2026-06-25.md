# Immigration–Crime Frontier Scout (2026-06-25)

**Scope:** Recent (2023–2026) immigration-crime literature + crime datasets BEYOND what the repo already built/fetched. Axis = CRIME RATES, victimization/underreporting, recidivism, meta-evidence + its disconfirmation. Enforcement→crime is the POLICY agent's axis (excluded here).

**Instrument-bias frame:** politically charged, LLM-assisted, left-tilted field. Repo prior = "immigrants commit LESS crime" (well-supported, in-data). Per constitution: actively seek strongest evidence AGAINST that prior; steelman before critique; disconfirmation mandatory.

**Already in-data / being fetched elsewhere — NOT recounted as new:** Light-He-Robey 2020 (TX DPS), BJS SPI 2016, SCAAP, Abramitzky 2024 incarceration gap, homicide-disentangle TX 2020, Ousey-Kubrin 2018 meta, the 188-spec curve, all enforcement/sanctuary nulls.

## (a) Papers — grades + what each tests/disconfirms

### Crime-rate / offending by status (2023–2026) — NEW frontier

**Marie & Pinotti 2024, "Immigration and Crime: An International Perspective," JEP 38(1):181–200** [SOURCE: https://www.aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/jep.38.1.181 — DOI 10.1257/jep.38.1.181] **GRADE: A (top-field review, complimentary full PDF).** The single most important *new* synthesis the repo lacks. Tests the central question with NEW international admin data + reviews causal econometric literature. KEY DISCONFIRMING-OF-NAIVE-PRIOR nuance: immigrants ARE "disproportionately represented among offenders in numerous host countries" (younger/male/less-educated composition) — YET immigration has a NULL effect on local crime rates, AND legalization REDUCES offending. This is the honest steelman: the raw overrepresentation among offenders in Europe is real; it's the *causal rate effect* and *composition-adjusted* picture that is null/negative. Reconciles the apparent paradox the repo's "less crime" framing can paper over. [FRAMING-SENSITIVE: "overrepresented among offenders" vs "lower rates" are both true and depend on raw-vs-adjusted, US-vs-Europe.]

**Light, Anadon (& earlier Light-He-Robey) — NIJ Final Report 308552 (2022, pub. surfaced 2024), "Unauthorized Immigration, Crime, and Recidivism: Evidence from Texas"** [SOURCE: https://nij.ojp.gov/library/publications/unauthorized-immigration-crime-and-recidivism-evidence-texas + grant PDF https://www.ojp.gov/pdffiles1/nij/grants/308552.pdf] **GRADE: A- (federal-funded, full 79pp, CA+TX).** Extends the in-data Light 2020 PNAS with (i) a CALIFORNIA replication (gap LARGER in CA than TX), (ii) immigration-homicide nexus empirics by race/national-origin (Asian-origin especially low), (iii) explicit RECIDIVISM section. **The recidivism finding is a genuine disconfirmation-of-our-own-confidence:** they CANNOT cleanly estimate recidivism because criminal-justice DBs rarely record whether the defendant was *deported* — so apparent "non-recidivism" may be removal-from-risk-set, not desistance. This is the cleanest statement of the recidivism identification problem; it cuts AGAINST over-claiming low immigrant recidivism. [This is the answer to the parent's "recidivism by status" axis — the honest answer is "not identified."]

**Eno Louden et al. — NIJ 309524 (2024) + Psychology, Public Policy & Law (2026, manuscript 2026-50707-001), El Paso jail booking + Central Eight risk study** [SOURCE: https://www.ojp.gov/pdffiles1/nij/grants/309524.pdf + https://psycnet.apa.org/manuscript/2026-50707-001.pdf] **GRADE: B+ (individual-level, jail-booking cross-section, N=5,175 intakes + 273 risk-assessed; single-site El Paso).** NEW mechanism evidence: undocumented immigrants score LOWER on 7 of 8 "Central Eight" criminogenic risk factors, and higher acculturation → higher risk (the assimilation-to-mean pattern). Most (58%) of immigrant bookings were immigration-related offenses (illegal entry), not violence/DWI. Individual-level + risk-factor mechanism is a layer the repo's aggregate/arrest-rate evidence lacks.

**RTI/NIJ 2024 (NCJ via 310356), "Research into Immigration and Crime … Synthetic Population," Final Report (Dec 2024)** [SOURCE: https://nij.ojp.gov/library/publications/research-immigration-and-crime-advancing-understanding-immigration-crime-and + https://www.ojp.gov/pdffiles1/nij/grants/310356.pdf] **GRADE: B+ (10-jurisdiction, tract-level, synthetic-population estimate of UNAUTHORIZED share — the hardest measurement problem, addressed head-on).** Two findings cut DIFFERENT ways and matter for honesty: (1) **UNAUTHORIZED** immigrant population share shows **NO significant association** with arrest rates once known crime correlates + reporting are controlled (i.e., the protective effect is carried by AUTHORIZED immigrants; unauthorized is a null, not a negative, at the ecological level). (2) AUTHORIZED immigration → lower drug/property/violent crime, confirming prior. (3) The first-gen advantage **diminishes by the 2nd generation** (children of immigrants arrested at rates similar to children of natives). This is the strongest in-genre statement that the "immigrants less crime" result is (a) documentation-status-heterogeneous and (b) generationally decaying — both honest caveats. [FRAMING-SENSITIVE]

**Tufail, Song, Ali, Wahab, Hassan 2023, "Does more immigration lead to more violent and property crimes? 30 OECD countries 1988–2018," Economic Research-Ekonomska Istraživanja 36(1):1867–1885** [SOURCE: https://ideas.repec.org/a/taf/reroxx/v36y2023i1p1867-1885.html] **GRADE: C+ (cross-country panel, lower-tier journal, endogeneity not fully addressed).** Cross-national panel: no statistically significant immigration→crime link; weak negative on one of six crime types; more foreign *prisoners* → less crime (a mechanically-suspect result worth flagging). Useful as triangulating cross-national evidence but methodologically the weakest of the rate studies — do not over-weight.

**"Four Pillars of the Immigration-Crime Myth," Social Sciences 14(12):709 (Dec 2025)** [SOURCE: https://www.mdpi.com/2076-0760/14/12/709] **GRADE: C (advocacy-framed review + 2025 Lucid opinion survey; MDPI; the title itself signals a frame).** Newest (2025) review-style piece; useful only as a map of the consensus claims ("~100 studies," Ousey-Kubrin anchor) and explicitly NAMES its frame (debunking a "myth"). [FRAMING-SENSITIVE: this is an advocacy framing — cite for the literature map, NOT as independent evidence; it is exactly the left-tilt the constitution warns the instrument shares.]

### Victimization & underreporting (the chilling-effect line)

**Xie & Baumer 2021, "Immigrant status, citizenship, and victimization risk … new findings from NCVS," Criminology 59 (DOI 10.1111/1745-9125.12278)** [SOURCE: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/1745-9125.12278 + PMC8849556] **GRADE: A- (first national victimization-by-citizenship analysis, 2017–2018 NCVS, the data infrastructure that newly enabled this).** Foreign-born status (but NOT acquired US citizenship) confers protection AGAINST being victimized; protection does NOT extend to "ambiguous citizenship status" respondents (whose profile matches undocumented). First rigorous national answer to "are immigrants more victimized?" — no, foreign-born are LESS victimized, but the likely-undocumented subgroup loses that protection. Bears directly on the underreporting→victimization mechanism.

**Cato 2025 (D. Bier / A. Nowrasteh), "Immigrants Cut Victimization Rates, Boost Crime Reporting"** [SOURCE: https://www.cato.org/policy-analysis/immigrants-cut-victimization-rates-boost-crime-reporting, pub 2025-08-26] **GRADE: B (think-tank, but transparent NCVS 2017–2023 pooled analysis, replicable; right-leaning Cato — note it CUTS WITH the prior, against its usual prior, strengthening credibility).** FRESHEST victimization evidence: immigrants 44% less likely than US-born to be violently victimized; 64% less likely to be harmed by someone known; and — against the chilling-effect story — immigrant victims 29% MORE likely to personally report violent crime to police (5.1M crimes, ~half-million arrests). **CRITICAL LIMITATION (stated by Cato itself):** NCVS does NOT separately identify illegal immigrants; "noncitizens" pools legal + illegal, and there is no valid way to isolate undocumented in NCVS. So this rebuts chilling-effect for IMMIGRANTS-AS-A-WHOLE but cannot speak to the undocumented-only subgroup — exactly where Comino/Jácome/Gonçalves find the chilling effect bites. [FRAMING-SENSITIVE: "immigrants report MORE" (Cato, pooled) vs "undocumented report LESS / are chilled" (Gonçalves-Jácome-Weisburst, Comino) are NOT contradictory — different populations. This reconciliation is the key honest point.]

**Gonçalves, Jácome & Weisburst 2024, "Immigration Enforcement and Public Safety," NBER w32109** [SOURCE: https://www.nber.org/system/files/working_papers/w32109/w32109.pdf] **GRADE: A- (NBER, national NCVS, Secure Communities design).** *Borders the policy agent's axis but the VICTIMIZATION result is ours:* Secure Communities reduced Hispanic crime REPORTING by ~9 pp (30% drop) AND raised Hispanic VICTIMIZATION — total reported crimes unchanged, masking the two opposing effects. This is the mechanism by which enforcement-driven underreporting BIASES measured offending: if undocumented victims stop reporting, measured "crime" understates harm done TO them. Directly relevant to whether arrest/report-based offending measures are biased.

[NOTE — DACA crime-reporting (Pinedo, JPubE 2024, S0094119024000408): DACA eligibility raised victim reporting 8.3pp/20.6%; same chilling-effect family, confirms legalization→more reporting. Already adjacent to policy-agent's DACA coverage; logged for completeness.]

## (d) Disconfirmation — methodological soft spots of the "less crime" finding (weighted honestly)

Per constitution: actively sought the STRONGEST evidence AGAINST the prior. Verdict: **the prior survives, but with three genuine, non-trivial caveats and one decisively-rebutted challenge.**

**THE STRONGEST CHALLENGE — and why it fails:** Lott & Wang / CPRC (SSRN 3099992), AZ prison data 1985–2017: undocumented "at least 142% more likely to be convicted," 8% of prison vs 2% of pop, more gang membership, more serious crimes. This is the ONE major published study finding the OPPOSITE direction, and it uses conviction (not self-report) admin data — superficially strong. **It is rebutted by a documented coding error** [SOURCE: verify_claim verdict "supported", confidence 1.0; https://www.cato.org/blog/responding-john-r-lott-jr-illegal-immigrant-criminality + factcheck.org 2018]: the AZ DOC "non-U.S. citizen and deportable" variable Lott treats as "undocumented" actually captures BOTH legal and undocumented non-citizens — **confirmed by the AZ DOC itself**. Correcting it attenuates/reverses the result (undocumented incarceration share falls toward/below population share). Lott disputes the rebuttal (crimeresearch.org), so it's a live dispute, but the weight of reanalysis + the agency's own classification statement is decisive. *This is the textbook steelman-then-falsify the constitution demands — and a caution that admin-status fields are easy to miscode.*

**THREE CAVEATS THAT GENUINELY SOFTEN THE PRIOR (honest weighting):**
1. **Documentation-status heterogeneity — the protective effect may be carried by AUTHORIZED immigrants.** NIJ/RTI 2024 synthetic-population study finds UNAUTHORIZED share is a NULL ecological predictor of arrests once controlled; the negative association is driven by AUTHORIZED immigrants. Individual-level TX/AZ data still show undocumented BELOW natives, but the ecological "immigrants reduce crime" claim should NOT be assumed to run through the undocumented specifically. [MODERATE weight — ecological null ≠ individual elevation.]
2. **Generational decay.** Multiple sources (RTI 2024; El Paso acculturation finding; the "Central Eight rises with acculturation" result) converge: the first-gen advantage shrinks by the 2nd generation toward native rates. The "immigrants less crime" finding is a FIRST-GENERATION fact, not a permanent ethnic one. [STRONG weight — well-replicated.]
3. **Specific-offense / specific-origin elevations exist within the aggregate.** Light-Miller-Kelly (PMC6241529) note Green (2016): unauthorized immigration from MEXICO specifically may associate with higher violence even where the aggregate is null. Aggregates can mask heterogeneity by origin/offense. [MODERATE weight — single cross-sectional study, but a real flag against over-aggregation.]

**DETECTION-BIAS runs in BOTH directions (net ambiguous, slight pro-prior tilt):**
- AGAINST prior: undocumented may be UNDER-arrested because they avoid police contact / aren't in DBs → arrest-based rates understate true offending. (The honest worry.)
- FOR prior: undocumented are OVER-exposed to detection (ICE/Secure Communities data-sharing, document checks, the very status check that records them) and have the MOST to lose from any arrest → strong incentive to avoid crime; and conviction (Nowrasteh TX homicide, the highest-quality measure) confirms the gap on the offense LEAST subject to reporting bias (homicide is near-fully-reported). The homicide-conviction result (illegal 26% below native-born, TX 2013–22) is the disconfirmation-resistant core: it doesn't depend on victims reporting or on minor-crime status-coding (which Nowrasteh himself flags as "suspect" for non-homicide TX convictions).

**Underreporting BIASES the victimization story, not the offending story, toward the prior:** the chilling effect (Gonçalves-Jácome-Weisburst; Comino) means undocumented are MORE victimized and report LESS — so measured crime understates harm done TO them, and Cato's "immigrants report MORE" (pooled, includes naturalized) does NOT generalize to undocumented. Don't conflate the two populations.

**BOTTOM LINE:** The "immigrants (esp. first-generation) commit less crime than the US-born" finding is robust on the highest-quality measures (TX homicide CONVICTIONS, 150-yr incarceration series, NCVS victimization). The defensible weakenings are: (a) it's a first-gen, not permanent, fact; (b) at the ECOLOGICAL level the protective signal is carried by authorized, not undocumented, immigrants (undocumented ≈ null); (c) origin/offense heterogeneity is real. The one direction-reversing study (Lott AZ) rests on a coding error the agency itself contradicts. No 2023–26 work overturns the consensus; the only formal quantitative meta-analysis remains Ousey-Kubrin 2018 (negative-but-weak) — newer pieces are narrative reviews (Marie-Pinotti JEP 2024, Kubrin-Alvarado 2025).
