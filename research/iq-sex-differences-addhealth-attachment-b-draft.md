## Draft Short Project Description

This project studies how transcript-coded course structure, transcript-coded academic evaluation, and curriculum exposure relate to later educational outcomes in the same respondents. Public-use analyses already show that school grades and tested achievement do not behave as one interchangeable construct. The requested restricted-use `Add Health` and `AHAA` files are limited to the minimum transcript-linked components needed to distinguish transcript-coded course structure, transcript-coded academic evaluation, curriculum exposure, school context, and later educational attainment. The project is not designed to estimate a generic “sex difference in intelligence”; it is designed to identify which specific school-pipeline channels account for the observed divergence between tested achievement surfaces and school-evaluated performance surfaces.

[INFERENCE]

---

## Draft Why Restricted Data Are Needed

Restricted-use transcript-linked data are required because the public `Add Health` files do not contain the transcript-coded course sequence, transcript-coded credits and failures, curriculum summaries, transcript timing/linking indicators, or transcript weights needed to separate exposure, evaluation, and curriculum channels. Without the `AHAA` transcript layer, public grades remain an evaluation surface and cannot adjudicate the live mechanism question.

[INFERENCE]

## Draft Research Questions

1. Do transcript-coded math/science course structure and transcript-coded GPA explain different parts of the later school-performance wedge? [INFERENCE]
2. Does curriculum exposure compress the divergence between tested achievement surfaces and school-evaluated performance surfaces more than grades alone do? [INFERENCE]
3. Conditional on baseline background and tested anchors, do transcript-coded course structure, transcript-coded GPA, and curriculum exposure predict later educational attainment differently by sex? [INFERENCE]

## Draft Narrow Justification By File

### `edumsov1` / `edumsov2` / `edu1`

These files are required to recover transcript-coded course structure, course performance, failure, and credit intensity. They are necessary to distinguish transcript exposure from transcript evaluation. [INFERENCE]

### `edutmsum` / `edutssum`

These files are required to measure curriculum exposure rather than relying on course titles alone. They are necessary to test whether the remaining school-performance divergence tracks actual curriculum content. [INFERENCE]

### `edugrad`

This file is required to connect transcript-coded high-school progression to completion / exit outcomes. [INFERENCE]

### `eduocr`

This file is required to avoid overreading transcript differences as purely individual by adding school-level context. [INFERENCE]

### `eduwgt`

This file is required for defensible inference because the transcript sample is not self-weighting. [INFERENCE]

## Draft “Why Not Request More?”

The request is intentionally narrow. It does not ask for every available contextual, neighborhood, or auxiliary education file. The requested files are limited to the minimum transcript-linked components needed to identify transcript course structure, transcript evaluation, curriculum exposure, and later attainment in the same respondents.
