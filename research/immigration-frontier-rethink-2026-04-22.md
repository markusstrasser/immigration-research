# Immigration frontier rethink — 2026-04-22

**Question:** After the county `flow/capacity` falsification pass weakened the annual wage/employment causal reading, what is the right next frontier?  
**Tier:** Deep | **Date:** 2026-04-22  
**Ground truth:** The annual county panel still supports `flow/capacity` as a structured stress marker, but it no longer cleanly identifies the wage channel and it leaves employment materially confounded. [SOURCE: research/immigration-capacity-falsification-2026-04-21.md]  
**Method note:** This memo is a zoomed-out reframe, not a new causal estimate. It ranks better research objects after the annual county panel hit diminishing returns. [INFERENCE]

## Bottom line

The right move is to **demote the county annual panel** from would-be causal proof to `screening surface`.

The next frontier is not “more threshold search.” It is three different objects:

1. `system saturation` at the receiver-node level
2. `resident-weighted incidence` instead of aggregate or median-county summaries
3. `bounds and kill tests` for the strongest claims where point identification is weak

That is the main rethink. [INFERENCE]

## Why the old frontier is now close to exhausted

The corrected falsification pass already forced the key downgrade:

1. the descriptive `flow/capacity` signal survives strongly [SOURCE: research/immigration-capacity-falsification-2026-04-21.md]
2. the clean annual wage windows do not line up, so the annual county panel does not cleanly identify the wage channel [SOURCE: research/immigration-capacity-falsification-2026-04-21.md]
3. the threshold search beats a null search for `wage`, but the cutoff surface is diffuse and transfer to `margin` is poor [SOURCE: research/immigration-capacity-falsification-2026-04-21.md]
4. the newcomer comparison exposed another measurement problem: median-county descriptive ratios do not answer typical resident exposure cleanly [SOURCE: .model-review/2026-04-22-immigration-capacity-falsification-close-9b3c1e/verified-disposition.md]

So one more county threshold variant is much more likely to create false precision than new truth. [INFERENCE]

## The better frontier

### 1) Receiver-node saturation

The clearest real-world failure modes already sit at concrete nodes:

1. shelter stock
2. hotel overflow
3. school intake
4. court backlog
5. emergency procurement
6. clinic and social-service intake

This means the better causal unit is often not `county` and not `% foreign-born`. It is `node load relative to node capacity over time`. [INFERENCE]

**Why this is better:** the repo's strongest concrete crisis cases were receiver-city service and shelter strain, not generic county employment collapse. [SOURCE: research/immigration-causal-surge-2021-2024.md] [SOURCE: research/immigration-threshold-causal-levers-2026-04-21.md]

### 2) Resident-weighted local incidence

The local-incumbent question is not answered by:

1. aggregate GDP
2. median county ratios
3. mixed-universe domestic newcomer series

It is answered by resident-weighted exposure:

1. renter exposure
2. homeowner exposure
3. parent-of-school-age-child exposure
4. low-education worker exposure
5. taxpayer exposure

This is where the current measurement stack is still weak. [INFERENCE]

**Why this is better:** the latest critique correctly flagged that median-county newcomer summaries can misstate typical resident exposure. [SOURCE: .model-review/2026-04-22-immigration-capacity-falsification-close-9b3c1e/verified-disposition.md]

### 3) Bounds and kill tests

Some headline disputes can be narrowed decisively without pretending we have perfect causal identification.

The two strongest examples are:

1. `global gains` survive only if destination-capacity erosion stays below a break-even bound [SOURCE: research/immigration-open-borders-double-world-gdp-and-apartheid-audit-2026-04-21.md]
2. `local resident harm` survives only if resident-weighted incidence remains after reweighting and after institution-specific load measures replace blunt county stock measures [INFERENCE]

This is the right place for math and logic when the public data cannot cleanly identify everything. [INFERENCE]

## Ranked next research objects

### Rank 1: receiver failure atlas

Build a cross-city `failure atlas` for:

1. `NYC`
2. `Denver`
3. `Chicago`
4. `Boston`
5. `Bexar`
6. one low-drama comparison city

Track:

1. load
2. capacity stock
3. duration above capacity
4. overflow behavior
5. emergency fiscal response
6. political response

This is the best empirical object because it studies the failures the repo already thinks are real. [INFERENCE]

### Rank 2: resident-weighted exposure repair

Replace median-county newcomer framing with:

1. population-weighted exposure
2. renter-weighted exposure
3. student-weighted exposure
4. low-skill-labor-force-weighted exposure

This is the fastest high-value repair because the current critique has already shown why the county-median framing is not enough. [SOURCE: .model-review/2026-04-22-immigration-capacity-falsification-close-9b3c1e/verified-disposition.md]

### Rank 3: assignment-shock / regime split

Treat these as first-order causes:

1. right-to-shelter rules
2. bus-routing and placement shocks
3. hotel contracting
4. school-assignment obligations
5. court or asylum-processing backlog

That is likely a cleaner path to causal leverage than another ACS-stock proxy regression. [INFERENCE]

### Rank 4: failure-mode atlas

Stop insisting on one unified effect. Model:

1. housing
2. shelter
3. schools
4. courts
5. labor
6. politics

as distinct failure modes with different breakpoints and different timing. [INFERENCE]

### Rank 5: global-gains break-even math

For the strongest open-borders rhetoric, the key inequality is:

`global gains survive iff place-premium gains > destination-capacity losses + origin losses`

That does not settle the exact number, but it does force the argument into a falsifiable form. [INFERENCE]

## What to stop doing

1. Stop treating `% immigrant` or `% foreign-born` as the master threshold variable. The repo's own work already moved toward `flow x capacity x regime`. [SOURCE: research/immigration-capacity-frontier-2026-04-21.md]
2. Stop treating annual county wages as the court of last appeal. The clean annual presurge windows no longer support that confidence level. [SOURCE: research/immigration-capacity-falsification-2026-04-21.md]
3. Stop using mixed-universe domestic newcomer counts as if they were native-incumbent sorting. The repo already corrected that. [SOURCE: research/immigration-reasoning-evolution-2026-04-21.md]
4. Stop searching for one universal county breakpoint. The threshold surface is too diffuse for that rhetoric. [SOURCE: research/immigration-capacity-falsification-2026-04-21.md]

## Best current formulation

The repo's current best position after the rethink is:

1. `county flow/capacity` is a strong descriptive stress marker [SOURCE: research/immigration-capacity-falsification-2026-04-21.md]
2. `county annual wage/employment causality` is still not settled [SOURCE: research/immigration-capacity-falsification-2026-04-21.md]
3. `receiver-node saturation` is the most promising next causal object [INFERENCE]
4. `resident-weighted incidence` is the most important next measurement repair [INFERENCE]
5. `bounds and necessary-condition tests` are the right way to make some claims more definitive without fake precision [INFERENCE]
