# Immigration epistemic check

Date: 2026-04-10

## What looks sound

1. `county-first bridge` is the right compromise.
Reason: it is less precise than district assignment, but it is honest about what public-use ACS geography can support.

2. `federal vs local incidence split` should be treated as a core result, not a side note.
Reason: that is where the evidence base and the current warehouse align best.

3. `household-normalized child burden` is a necessary correction.
Reason: the earlier adult-linked proxy was structurally wrong.

## What is shaky

1. `area-weighted PUMA-to-county crosswalk` is usable but weak.
Reason: land area is not population, so it should stay labeled as a first-pass local-context bridge, not a precise exposure model.

2. `school-spend-per-pupil` is only a pressure proxy.
Reason: spending levels do not equal marginal burden, quality effects, or district assignment.

3. `CHAS Table 11` is a good stress indicator, not a welfare scalar.
Reason: it captures housing problems, but not the full incidence split between renters, owners, landlords, and builders.

4. `IRS county migration balance` is contextual, not causal.
Reason: it says something about local mobility and tax-base motion, but not immigrant-specific burden by itself.

## Claims that should be relabeled

1. Replace `is low-skill immigration bad for a country?` with:
`Under explicit AGI timelines and counterfactual policy choices, which low-skill immigration flows improve or degrade institutional resilience, local-system capacity, and net citizen welfare?`

2. Replace `average citizen` with an incidence table.
Reason: that phrase hides too much distribution.

3. Replace any implicit `national net negative` claim with `local-burden-heavy in specific origin x destination x household cells, with national sign still framing-sensitive`.

## AGI-soon implication

If `AGI soon` is a live scenario, the central object shifts away from lifetime-average immigrant NPV and toward:
1. short-run transition costs,
2. automation complementarity versus delay,
3. institutional resilience,
4. option value under radical uncertainty.

This is a real reframing, not a footnote.
