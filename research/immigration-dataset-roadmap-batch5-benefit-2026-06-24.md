# Immigration — Dataset Roadmap, Batch 5: Benefit / Economic-Contribution side (2026-06-24)

Fifth batch. Supplements the **45 datasets** already proposed across
`immigration-dataset-roadmap.md` (12), `-additions-2026-06-24.md` (6), `-batch3-2026-06-24.md` (14+3),
`-batch4-us-2026-06-24.md` (7), `-batch4-intl-2026-06-24.md` (6), and the held stack in
`immigration-dataset-register.md`. **None of these repeats the 45 already on the books.**

**Axis = the economic-benefit / contribution side** — the half the corpus's own audit
(`immigration-evidence-base-audit.md`) flags as under-weighted vs the cost-side ledger. Targeted gaps,
in priority order requested by parent:
(1) **high-skill** — NSF NSCG (immigrant scientists/engineers + earnings), NSF SED (foreign-born PhD production + stay rates);
(2) **foreign students as fiscal contributors** — SEVIS/SEVP + IIE Open Doors (tuition/spending magnitude);
(3) **consumption / local demand** — BLS Consumer Expenditure Survey (immigrant household spending — the direct counter to the remittance-leakage frame already on the roadmap as WB-KNOMAD-REMIT);
(4) **entrepreneurship / firm formation** — Census Business Formation Statistics (BFS), Kauffman Indicators (the *new-firm-flow* angle distinct from the already-listed ABS-CBO owner-*stock*).

All URLs verified live via search 2026-06-24 unless tagged `[UNVERIFIED]`. Join-key legend for the
warehouse: `state_fips`, `county_fips`, `POBP`/origin-country, `education_bucket`, `citizenship`/`nativity`,
`field_of_study`, `FY/year`. A dataset with no nativity/status field is flagged as an ecological/calibration layer.

**De-duplication note — what is ALREADY covered on this axis (do NOT re-card):**

## Findings (appended as verified)

## Axis 1 — High-skill (immigrant scientists/engineers + foreign PhD production) — highest priority

### NSF-NSCG — National Survey of College Graduates (immigrant scientists/engineers + earnings)
**Source:** NSF National Center for Science and Engineering Statistics (NCSES), conducted by the US Census Bureau · **Access:** PUBLIC — direct compressed `.zip` public-use microdata (no registration), SAS/Stata/CSV; fully scriptable from stable ncses.nsf.gov paths. Also re-distributed as harmonized extracts via **IPUMS Higher Ed** (highered.ipums.org). Restricted-use (state, institution, longitudinal links) via NCSES SDAF enclave or FSRDC.
**Official:** public-use file index https://ncses.nsf.gov/explore-data/microdata/national-survey-college-graduates · survey hub https://ncses.nsf.gov/surveys/national-survey-college-graduates · Census mirror https://www.census.gov/programs-surveys/nscg.html · interactive Data Explorer https://ncsesdata.nsf.gov/explorer?survey=NSCG · IPUMS Higher Ed https://highered.ipums.org/highered/ [SOURCE: ncses.nsf.gov/explore-data/microdata/national-survey-college-graduates]
**Format / size:** biennial public-use microdata, every cycle **1993 → 2023** (latest); per-year `.zip` ~28–85 MB (College_Grads_2023 = 61.2 MB). Population represented = 71.7 M individuals with ≥ bachelor's, <76, US/PR resident; survey conducted since the 1970s.
**Key variables — DOES IT HAVE NATIVITY? YES.** The Data Explorer lists **Citizenship** as a named Demographics topic (1993–2023), alongside **place of birth / country of birth**, **year of entry to the US**, and **type of visa at most recent entry** on the questionnaire. Plus: **salary/earnings**, field of highest degree, occupation (the unique degree-field × occupation cross), employment sector, work activities, R&D involvement, job satisfaction, student-loan debt, work-related training, age/sex/race. The published data tables already cross full-time earnings by occupation × citizenship status (e.g. 2019 Table 2-3).
**Why it fits:** THE federal microdata source for the **high-skill immigrant contribution** — it measures foreign-born scientists' and engineers' **earnings, fields, and occupations** at the person level, the benefit channel the corpus under-weights (its high-skill evidence is the secondary Bernstein-et-al patent citation and ABS owner counts; NSCG is the direct labor-market earnings micro). Lets the project quantify the immigrant share of the STEM workforce and the foreign-born earnings premium/parity by field — a direct counter to the "low-skill-drag" framing by showing the high-skill arm's tax-base contribution. Biennial 30-year series; free, scriptable; harmonized panel via IPUMS Higher Ed.
**Limit / honesty:** the public file has **NO state or sub-national geography** (region/division only; state + institution are restricted via SDAF/FSRDC); the SDAF version adds longitudinal links (2010+) but not the restricted geo. "College graduates residing in the US during the reference week" → it is a **stock of the foreign-born already here with a US-recognized degree**, not a flow or an arrival panel, and **misses immigrants whose foreign credential isn't captured as a US degree-equivalent in some cycles** (frame coverage of foreign degrees has varied). Cross-sectional within most cycles (the panel is restricted). No legal-status detail beyond citizenship/visa-at-entry; no unauthorized (by construction — degree-holders). [SOURCE: ncses.nsf.gov/surveys/national-survey-college-graduates]
**Graph fit:** new `high_skill_workforce` layer; `citizenship`/place-of-birth/visa-at-entry × field-of-degree × occupation × earnings × year; origin-country joins the origin layer; region-level only (no `state_fips` on public file); the person-level high-skill benefit counterpart to ABS-CBO (owners) and USPTO (inventors).

### NSF-SED — Survey of Earned Doctorates (foreign-born PhD production + stay rates)
**Source:** NSF NCSES (co-sponsored by NIH, Dept. of Education, NEH), conducted by RTI International · **Access:** PUBLIC for the **data tables + interactive tools**; the SED **person-level microdata is RESTRICTED** (no public-use file). Free tiers: per-year Data Tables (Excel/PDF .zip), the interactive **SED data builder** (ncsesdata.nsf.gov/builder/sed), and the **SED Restricted Data Analysis System (RDAS)** online (custom tables, 2017+ variables, secured platform — generate tables without a license). Person-level microdata only via FSRDC/license.
**Official:** survey hub https://ncses.nsf.gov/surveys/earned-doctorates/2024 · 2024 Data Tables report (NSF 25-349) https://ncses.nsf.gov/pubs/nsf25349/assets/nsf25349.pdf · "Doctorate Recipients from U.S. Universities: 2023" report https://ncses.nsf.gov/pubs/nsf25300/assets/report/nsf25300-report.pdf · SED data builder https://ncsesdata.nsf.gov/builder/sed · RDAS https://ncsesdata.nsf.gov/rdas · stay-rate analysis (NSF 25-325) https://ncses.nsf.gov/pubs/nsf25325 · SED info site https://sedsurvey.org/ [SOURCE: ncses.nsf.gov/surveys/earned-doctorates/2024]
**Format / size:** **annual census** (not a sample) of ALL research-doctorate recipients from accredited US institutions, **academic year 1958 → 2024** (~50,000 graduates/yr); data tables as Excel/PDF, ~5–8 MB per annual release.
**Key variables — DOES IT HAVE NATIVITY? YES — citizenship is a core dimension.** Citizenship status (US citizen / permanent resident / temporary visa holder) × **broad and major field of doctorate** (trend tables back to 1977–79); country/economy of citizenship; **median basic annual salary** of those with definite non-postdoc employment ($104,000 for S&E in 2024); definite postgraduation commitments, employment sector, time-to-degree, graduate funding source, education debt, parental education. **The standout: foreign-doctorate STAY RATES** — what share of temporary-visa S&E PhDs remain in the US ~5 yr (short-term) and ~10 yr (long-term) after graduation, by field and origin country (the actual stay-rate figures come from the *Survey of Doctorate Recipients* follow-up; SED supplies the intentions + denominator). 2017–23: **73.2% of temporary-visa doctorate recipients intend to stay**; long-term stay rates: China 85%, India 75%, Iran 96%, by field Engineering 71–80%, Computer/Math 76%.
**Why it fits:** the canonical measure of the **brain-gain channel** — the US trains foreign nationals to the doctoral level and most STAY, a large benefit-side human-capital transfer the cost-focused stack entirely omits. SED gives both the **production** side (foreign-born share of US PhDs by field — historically >50% in engineering/CS/economics) and the **retention** side (stay rates), the two numbers that size how much the high-skill immigration channel adds to the US research workforce. Annual census (no sampling error on counts), 66-year series, free tables + RDAS for custom cuts. The "foreign-born PhD production + stay rates" gap, verified with the actual TAL-17/TAL-18/Table 2-8 numbers.
**Limit / honesty:** the **person-level microdata is NOT public** — you get pre-tabulated tables, the data builder, and RDAS (which generates custom tables but not a downloadable microdata file), so any cut NCSES hasn't pre-built requires RDAS or an FSRDC license. **Citizenship-AT-GRADUATION is not nativity-at-birth** and stay rates measure *intentions* (SED) refined by *observed residence* (SDR follow-up) — the SDR-derived stay rate is the firmer number; cite TAL-18 (observed) over Table 2-8 (intentions) where possible. "D" suppression on small country×field cells. Research doctorates only (not professional/master's/bachelor's high-skill immigration — pair with NSCG for the broader degreed workforce). [SOURCE: ncses.nsf.gov/pubs/nsf25300/assets/report/nsf25300-report.pdf]
**Graph fit:** `high_skill_workforce` / `brain_gain` layer; citizenship × field-of-doctorate × origin-country × year (counts + stay-rate + median salary); origin-country joins the origin layer; national (no within-US geo on public tables); the doctoral-production complement to NSCG's degreed-workforce stock.

## Axis 2 — Foreign students as fiscal contributors (tuition/spending magnitude + the contributor population)

### SEVP-SEVIS — Student & Exchange Visitor Program / SEVIS by the Numbers (the contributor population, admin)
**Source:** ICE Homeland Security Investigations, Student and Exchange Visitor Program (SEVP); the system of record is SEVIS · **Access:** PUBLIC — annual "SEVIS by the Numbers" report (PDF) + the **SEVIS Data Mapping Tool** which publishes **downloadable per-quarter data tables** (country-of-citizenship × academic-level × field × all-50-states), no registration; scriptable from stable studyinthestates.dhs.gov / ice.gov paths. The underlying SEVIS person-level records are restricted (FOIA/law-enforcement).
**Official:** 2024 report (PDF) https://www.ice.gov/doclib/sevis/btn/25_0605_2024-sevis-btn.pdf · report hub https://www.ice.gov/node/68373 · SEVIS Data Mapping Tool data (all quarters, downloadable tables) https://studyinthestates.dhs.gov/sevis-by-the-numbers/sevis-by-the-numbers-data · latest quarterly table (e.g. March 2026) https://studyinthestates.dhs.gov/sevis-data-mapping-tool/march-2026-sevis-data-mapping-tool-data · 2024 press release https://www.ice.gov/news/releases/ice-releases-2024-sevp-annual-report [SOURCE: ice.gov/doclib/sevis/btn/25_0605_2024-sevis-btn.pdf]
**Format / size:** annual report PDF (CY data) + quarterly Data Mapping Tool tables (HTML/CSV-extractable matrices, one row per country, columns = continent/region/active-students/M/F/each-degree-level/each-state). CY2024: **1,582,808 active F-1/M-1 records** from 229+ countries; quarterly back to April 2014; STEM-flagged variants. OPT counts reported separately.
**Key variables — DOES IT HAVE NATIVITY? YES, it IS the foreign-student universe.** Country of citizenship (the native key), academic level (associate / bachelor's / master's / doctorate / language / K-12 / vocational / flight), field of study, **state of enrollment (all 50 + territories)**, sex, SEVP-certified school. **The contribution-relevant standout: OPT / STEM-OPT work-authorization counts** — 381,140 unique students with practical-training work authorization in CY2024; 160,627 OPT students reported working for an employer in CY2023; STEM-OPT 122,101 in 2023 (39% India, 24% China). This is the foreign-student → US-labor-force bridge.
**Why it fits:** the **administrative ground-truth for the foreign-student contributor population** — F-1/M-1 students are tuition-importing, predominantly self/family-funded nonimmigrants (a benefit channel the cost stack omits entirely), and OPT/STEM-OPT is the mechanism by which they convert into the high-skill workforce (feeding the NSCG/SED high-skill layer and the H-1B channel in the proposed OFLC/USCIS cards). The state × country × level tables give the within-US geography to assign tuition/spending to receiving states. Quarterly, free, scriptable — and uniquely **the only one of the three foreign-student sources that is primary government admin data** (Open Doors is a voluntary institutional census; NAFSA is a derived estimate).
**Limit / honesty:** "active records" ≠ unique persons exactly (a student can have status changes); CY (calendar) basis differs from Open Doors' academic-year basis, so the two counts (1.58M SEVIS active vs 1.18M Open Doors enrolled) are NOT the same object — SEVIS counts all active F/M records incl. language/K-12/OPT-period, Open Doors counts degree-and-non-degree higher-ed enrollment. No dollars (SEVIS has no spending/tuition field — pair with Open Doors/NAFSA for magnitude). No earnings on OPT (only the count of work-authorized). Person-level is restricted; you get the published aggregate tables only. [SOURCE: ice.gov/news/releases/ice-releases-2024-sevp-annual-report]
**Graph fit:** new `foreign_students` layer; country-of-citizenship × academic-level × field × `state` × CY/quarter; country joins origin layer, `state` joins context layer; the primary-admin contributor-population base that Open Doors/NAFSA monetize.

### IIE-OPENDOORS + NAFSA-EVT — Open Doors enrollment census + NAFSA economic-value estimate (the magnitude + funding-source)
**Source:** (a) **IIE Open Doors** — Institute of International Education, in partnership with the US Dept. of State Bureau of Educational & Cultural Affairs (the official enrollment census since 1948); (b) **NAFSA International Student Economic Value Tool** — NAFSA + JB International (the dollar/jobs estimate built on Open Doors + US Dept. of Commerce data) · **Access:** PUBLIC — Open Doors data portal (downloadable data tables, no registration; the full annual dataset is purchasable but a decade of tables is free online); NAFSA tool is a free interactive (national / state / **congressional-district** tables, downloadable).
**Official:** Open Doors data portal https://opendoorsdata.org/data/international-students/ · Open Doors 2025 release https://www.iie.org/news/open-doors-2025-press-release/ · IIE economic-impact page https://www.iie.org/research-initiatives/open-doors/economic-impact-of-international-students/ · NAFSA Economic Value Tool https://www.nafsa.org/policy-and-advocacy/policy-resources/nafsa-international-student-economic-value-tool-v2 · NAFSA US fact sheet (state table) https://www.nafsa.org/sites/default/files/media/document/EconValue2025_v01_11-12-2025.pdf · NAFSA methodology (Baumgartner) https://www.nafsa.org/sites/default/files/media/document/NAFSA_Methodology_Economic_Value_2024_FINAL.pdf [SOURCE: iie.org/news/open-doors-2025-press-release/]
**Format / size:** Open Doors = downloadable data tables (enrollment, academic level, fields of study, places of origin, **primary source of funding**, leading host institutions, US states) — long series back to **1948/49**, latest 2024/25 (**1,177,766 students, 6% of US higher-ed**). NAFSA EVT = per-state + per-congressional-district dollar/jobs tables, annual.
**Key variables:** Open Doors — enrollment × academic level × field × **place of origin** × host state/institution × **primary source of funding** (the critical one: ~62% of international students are primarily funded by personal/family/home-country sources = imported money, not US support). NAFSA — **economic value in US dollars** ($42.9B in 2024-25, down 2% from the $43.8B 2023-24 record) and **jobs supported** (355,736), by state and congressional district; algorithm = enrolled students × (tuition+fees+room/board+living) − US-based support + dependents' living expenses. (Note: US Dept. of Commerce's own trade-services figure is higher, ~$55B in 2024, because it counts on a different basis.)
**Why it fits:** supplies the **dollar magnitude and the funding-source proof** that SEVIS lacks — the headline "$43B contribution / 6% of higher-ed / 62% foreign-funded" numbers that quantify the fiscal-contribution argument, with **state and congressional-district** granularity that joins the project's geographic ledger. The funding-source split is the direct rebuttal to "students are subsidized": most tuition is imported. Open Doors' 1948→ series is the long trend; NAFSA gives the spendable magnitude per state. Together they are the contribution-magnitude layer over SEVIS's contributor-population base.
**Limit / honesty — TIERING THE EVIDENCE:** Open Doors enrollment is a **voluntary institutional survey** (not a government census; ~3,000 institutions report) — solid but not admin-complete, and academic-year basis (differs from SEVIS CY). **NAFSA's $42.9B is a MODELED ESTIMATE, not measured spending** — it is an input-output-style imputation (enrollment × imputed cost − imputed support, then a jobs multiplier), an advocacy product by an association that lobbies for international education; treat the dollar figure as **`[FRAMING-SENSITIVE]` advocacy-modeled**, cross-check against the independent US Dept. of Commerce trade-in-services number (~$55B, also estimate-based), and do NOT present it as hard data. Gross spending ≠ net fiscal benefit (ignores any public subsidy to the enrolling institutions, infrastructure use). Neither has a tax-paid figure. The defensible hard cores are the Open Doors **enrollment counts + funding-source share**; the NAFSA dollar total is the contested headline. [SOURCE: nafsa.org/.../NAFSA_Methodology_Economic_Value_2024_FINAL.pdf]
**Graph fit:** `foreign_students` magnitude overlay; place-of-origin × academic-level × field × `state`/congressional-district × academic-year; origin + `state_fips` joins; monetizes the SEVP-SEVIS contributor base — but the dollar layer is modeled/advocacy, flagged distinct from the enrollment counts.
