# Immigration under AGI-soon reframing

Date: 2026-04-10

`[FRAMING-SENSITIVE]`

Assumption:
1. Treat `AGI soon` as a live policy-relevant scenario, roughly `2026-2040`.

## What becomes less important

1. Very long-run lifetime NPV arguments become less central.
Reason: if labor markets may be structurally rewritten before those horizons, long-run descendant-tax arithmetic carries less decision weight.

2. Simple `does immigration raise GDP?` arguments become less central.
Reason: under AGI-soon, resilience and transition management dominate level effects.

3. Long-run low-skill native wage-substitution fights become less central.
Reason: they still matter in transition, but not as the terminal object if broad automation compresses labor rents.

4. `Immigration fixes labor scarcity for decades` weakens as a standalone case.
Reason: machines may cover much of the long-run scarcity argument.

## What becomes more important

1. Short-run transition costs.
This includes local fiscal pressure, housing, schools, and system capacity before automation actually diffuses.

2. Aging and care bottlenecks as a bridge problem.
The question becomes whether immigration buys time before automation works at scale.

3. Political economy and legitimacy.
If voters face both automation anxiety and high immigration, backlash risk may dominate narrow wage arithmetic.

4. Automation complementarity versus delay.
The important margin is not only whether immigrants work, but whether their task mix speeds deployment or delays automation by making labor too cheap.

5. Option value under radical uncertainty.
`AGI soon` is a scenario, not a fact. Policy should prefer robustness across no-AGI, partial-AGI, and fast-AGI worlds.

## Better question

Replace:
1. `Are low-skill immigrants net contributors or net burdens?`

With:
1. `Given uncertainty about transformative automation between 2026 and 2040, which immigration margins reduce transition risk and improve national option value after accounting for local capacity costs, aging needs, political backlash, and the possibility that some labor inflows either complement or delay automation?`

Shorter version:
1. `The object is less lifetime average immigrant NPV, more transition management under radical uncertainty.`
