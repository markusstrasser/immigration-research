# IQ Sex Differences - Frontier Refresh 2026-03-06

**Date:** 2026-03-06  
**Purpose:** check whether the repo's strongest current claims are already explicit in the newest literature or whether the repo is still ahead mainly as an integrated synthesis.

## Bottom Line

Recent papers strengthen several component nodes the repo already carries:

1. `measurement / test design` matters
2. `effort / response process` matters
3. `evaluation / self-assessment` can diverge from objective math
4. `early-school emergence` is real
5. `tracking / system structure` plausibly amplifies gaps

But the frontier still mostly treats these as separate literatures. The repo’s main remaining novelty is still the integrated surface-family map, not a brand-new raw fact. [INFERENCE]

## What The Newest Papers Strengthen

### 1. Test format is not neutral

The strongest recent direct support for the measurement branch is:

- Silvia Griselda, *Gender gap in standardized tests: What are we measuring?* (2024), which uses random variation in `PISA` test format and finds that a `10` percentage-point increase in multiple-choice share inflates female under-performance in mathematics by `0.025` SD and male under-performance in reading by `0.035` SD. [SOURCE: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0167268124001033 ; mirrored abstract summary: https://econpapers.repec.org/article/eeejeborg/v_3a221_3ay_3a2024_3ai_3ac_3ap_3a191-229.htm]

This directly supports the repo’s `MeasurementSurface` node and the broader claim that “standardized math” is partly a format object, not just a pure latent-ability readout. [INFERENCE]

### 2. Time pressure is a real causal channel, but not the whole story

- *Gender Differences in Math Tests: the Role of Time Pressure* (Economic Journal, 2024) reports causal evidence from a randomized experiment: reducing or eliminating time pressure decreases the math gender gap by up to `40%`, with no loss in predictive correlation with university performance; the paper attributes most of the extra gap under time pressure to anxiety rather than strategy shifts. [SOURCE: https://academic.oup.com/ej/article/134/664/3461/7693116]

This strengthens the repo’s timing node. It does **not** overturn the repo’s latest `PISA` measurement read, because our local response-time work already shows broad female-slower timing does not line up tightly with the localized content-family score residuals. [INFERENCE]

### 3. Effort and nonresponse differences are real in low-stakes settings

- Marcq and Braeken (2024), *Gender differences in item nonresponse in the PISA 2018 student questionnaire*, finds that male students have greater item nonresponse propensity on the `PISA 2018` student questionnaire even after adjusting for position effects via a cross-classified logistic model. [SOURCE: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11092-023-09412-7]
- Borghans, Diris, and Tavares (2024), *Student characteristics and effort during test-taking*, finds girls exert more effort and show different effort decline over the test, with past attainment and personality also predictive. [SOURCE: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0959475224000513]

These strengthen the repo’s `behavior / compliance / stakes` node in the abstract, but not enough to rescue the strong internal `NLSY97` behavior story. They say broad effort differences are real. They do not show that broad effort differences are the dominant cause of the localized school-knowledge residuals we see. [INFERENCE]

### 4. Evaluation / self-assessment diverges from objective ability

- Shure et al. (2025), *Peers, parents, and self-perceptions: the gender gap in mathematics self-assessment*, shows that the gender gap in self-assessed mathematics ability persists after controlling for objective mathematics ability and fixed effects, and that parental and teacher assessments matter materially. [SOURCE: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00148-025-01087-2]

This does not directly prove transcript-grade bias. It does strengthen the repo’s broader `evaluation / label / self-concept` branch: school-linked and socially transmitted math surfaces are not reducible to objective math alone. [INFERENCE]

### 5. Early-school emergence remains real in new large cohorts

- Kuhfeld and Burchinal (2025), *Gender Gaps in the Early Grades: Questioning the Narrative that Schools Are Poorly Suited to Young Boys*, using nine cohorts and ten million students, reports that girls entered kindergarten with advantages in both subjects before the pandemic, but boys quickly surpassed girls in math during elementary school, while girls maintained a reading advantage. [SOURCE: https://edworkingpapers.com/ai25-1297]

This aligns with the repo’s child branch more than it conflicts with it. The exact magnitude and earliest sign still depend on scale family and anchor, but the presence of an early-school node is now even less disputable. [INFERENCE]

### 6. Education-system structure plausibly amplifies track-linked gaps

- Zapfe (2025), *The effect of the education system on the gender gaps in mathematics and reading competencies*, explicitly argues that early differentiation / tracking can widen gender gaps via gender-typed track placement, while standardization can moderate them. [SOURCE: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11614-025-00621-6]

This is consistent with the repo’s `TrackSelection` node and with the gap between broad school math and `TIMSS Advanced`. [INFERENCE]

## What The Literature Still Does Not Seem To Do Explicitly

I do **not** see a single recent paper that explicitly unifies:

- child `math versus verbal` bridge issues
- school-evaluation versus tested-math wedge
- localized school-knowledge anomalies like `Math Knowledge`
- item-family residual structure on `PISA`
- broad female-slower timing that does **not** fully explain the score residuals
- transition/adult `GPA versus tested-math` wedge

into one causal representation.

That remains the repo’s strongest candidate contribution. [INFERENCE]

## Updated Novelty Read

- `P(novel as isolated raw fact)`: still low, around `0.20` to `0.30`
- `P(novel as integrated cross-surface causal map)`: still high, around `0.75`

[INFERENCE]

The frontier increasingly validates the repo’s component nodes. What it still usually does **not** do is connect them into one inspectable, falsifiable, cross-dataset structure. [INFERENCE]

## Decision Impact

The newest literature makes one thing clearer:

The repo should stop spending time trying to rediscover whether timing, format, effort, or stereotypes matter in general. They do.

The frontier gap is now narrower:

1. formal latent-variable psychometrics on the local `PISA` branch
2. restricted transcript/test linkage on the school-wedge branch
3. a real mediator / original experiment if the goal is publication-grade causal evidence

[INFERENCE]
