## 5. My Top 5 Recommendations (different from the originals)

1. **What:** Rename the falsification family from `pretrend` to `lead-exposure presurge-window` unless both numerator and denominator are presurge.  
   **Why:** The biggest correctness issue is semantic, not computational. Right now the `2018–2019` window is clean, but the main exposure still uses a `2022` ACS-based recent-FB proxy. That can support a “were future high-load counties already weaker?” test, but not a clean presurge-treatment placebo. This is the highest-impact fix because it directly lowers false causal confidence at near-zero maintenance cost. [SOURCE: sources/immigration-causal/data/outcomes/analysis/county_capacity_falsification_summary.json]  
   **How to verify:**  
   - All output keys / memo text stop calling this a pure `pretrend` unless numerator vintage is `<2020`.  
   - Audit JSON gains fields `numerator_vintage` and `denominator_vintage`.  
   - Grep test: no uncaveated phrase `clean pretrend` remains in repo for this design.

2. **What:** Publish an exclusion audit for the `77` zero-prebase-permit counties and rerun one sensitivity that keeps them as a separate structural bin.  
   **Why:** `77/2390` counties are dropped from the presurge-load regressions, and they are not random missingness—they are the most denominator-fragile counties. That is exactly where threshold/capacity stories can be most distorted. [SOURCE: sources/immigration-causal/data/outcomes/analysis/county_capacity_falsification_summary.json]  
   **How to verify:**  
   - New table compares included vs excluded counties on region, population, recent-FB proxy, margin shift, rent, and current permits.  
   - Sensitivity model includes `zero_prebase_permit` dummy/bin.  
   - Track coefficient movement: if key betas move by `<20%`, current conclusions gain credibility; if `>=20%`, downgrade.

3. **What:** Add a supersession banner to `immigration-county-outcome-panel-2026-04-21.md` and strengthen the index note so readers cannot miss that the employment-null story was downgraded.  
   **Why:** The repo currently contains an older “wage not employment” memo alongside a newer falsification memo saying the wage/employment split is provisional and employment is more suspicious. This is a live documentation contradiction with high downstream supervision cost. [SOURCE: research/immigration-county-outcome-panel-2026-04-21.md] [SOURCE: research/immigration-capacity-falsification-2026-04-21.md]  
   **How to verify:**  
   - Banner text: `Superseded in part by immigration-capacity-falsification-2026-04-21.md`.  
   - Index consult-before text mentions both IRS quarantine **and** later downgrade of wage-vs-employment certainty.  
   - No current-state doc presents the old employment-null claim without caveat.

4. **What:** Formalize threshold diffuseness with concentration statistics, not just narrative language.  
   **Why:** The threshold surface’s modal actual cutoff is selected only `25%` of the time; null-search modal share reaches `16.7%`. That is weak absolute concentration. A statistic like entropy or HHI will make this impossible to overstate later. [SOURCE: sources/immigration-causal/data/outcomes/analysis/county_capacity_falsification_summary.json]  
   **How to verify:**  
   - Add `threshold_surface_concentration.json` with HHI, normalized entropy, modal share, top-3 share for actual and null.  
   - Success criterion for “stable location” should be explicit, e.g. `modal_share >= 0.5` or `HHI_actual - HHI_null >= 0.15`.  
   - Current tranche should fail that criterion and say so.

5. **What:** Extend the QCEW audit to state the exact handling of `disclosure_code == "N"` rows.  
   **Why:** The current audit says there are 4 nondisclosure rows across years but zero missing numeric cells. That may be fine, but it is opaque. This is a classic silent-semantic-failure zone: future readers cannot tell whether those rows were trusted, scrubbed, or accidentally passed through. [SOURCE: sources/immigration-causal/data/outcomes/county_outcome_panel_audit.json]

1. I may be overstating the “not a real pretrend” objection if your intended estimand is explicitly **future high-load counties vs earlier outcomes**, not a classic placebo. The artifact text still makes me think the semantics are too strong, but the underlying statistical object may be closer to what you wanted than my critique implies. [INFERENCE]

2. I may be too suspicious of the QCEW nondisclosure rows. County-total all-ownership, all-industry cells can sometimes remain numerically usable even when `disclosure_code == "N"`. Without the exact raw rows, I cannot prove mishandling—only that the audit is underspecified. [INFERENCE]

3. I may be under-crediting the threshold-null improvement if the omitted portion of `county_capacity_falsification_summary.json` shows a very large actual-vs-null sign-stability gap. My criticism is mainly about **location stability**, not necessarily **predictive performance**. [INFERENCE]

4. I may be over-indexing on public-argument provenance standards for a personal research repo. The chronology/provenance concerns matter if these files become public artifacts; they matter less if the human already knows the revision history. This is a known bias toward production-grade traceability. [TRAINING-DATA]
