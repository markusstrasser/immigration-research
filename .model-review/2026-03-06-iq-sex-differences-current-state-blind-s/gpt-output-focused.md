## 2. PISA Proxy Pass: Valid, Useful-But-Fragile, or Misleading?
Useful-but-fragile.

- It is valid for one narrow update: raw item-family gaps compress when you partial out a within-country `PISA` math surface. That is enough to weaken any “raw geometry alone proves unfairness” claim. ## 3. Early-School Alignment: What Is Actually Proven?
- Proven: direct raw-math comparisons overstated the `NLSCYA`/`ECLS` conflict. On a reading-anchored profile, `NLSCYA` is no longer cleanly opposite-signed to the `ECLS` cohorts. ([iq-sex-differences-early-school-score-alignment.md](/Users/alien/Projects/research/research/iq-sex-differences-early-school-score-alignment.md#L68))
- Not proven: a common early-math latent surface. The sign flip in `NLSCYA` comes from reading being more female-leaning than math, not from math itself becoming clearly male-leaning. ([early_school_alignment_models.tsv](/Users/alien/Projects/research/sources/iq-sex-diff/data/early_school_alignment/early_school_alignment_models.tsv#L15), [iq-sex-differences-early-school-score-alignment.md](/Users/alien/Projects/research/research/iq-sex-differences-early-school-score-alignment.md#L114))

In the age-matched bridge, the residual `NLSCYA` vs `ECLS-K:2011` gap is still large, about `0.18` to `0.49` SD depending on bin, and most `NLSCYA` conditional betas still cross zero. ([early_school_age_matched_compare.tsv](/Users/alien/Projects/research/sources/iq-sex-diff/data/early_school_alignment/early_school_age_matched_compare.tsv#L2), [early_school_age_matched_models.tsv](/Users/alien/Projects/research/sources/iq-sex-diff/data/early_school_alignment/early_school_age_matched_models.tsv#L13))

## 4. School-Surface Wedge: What Alternative Explanations Are Still Under-modeled?
- Contemporaneous curriculum exposure in `NLSY97`. The cleanest rival to an evaluation wedge is that `Math Knowledge` tracks current grade gate, recent coursework, or honors exposure; those fields are already loaded but omitted from the discriminator models. ([nlsy97_piat_cat_pass.py](/Users/alien/Projects/research/sources/iq-sex-diff/nlsy97_piat_cat_pass.py#L20), [nlsy97_piat_cat_pass.py](/Users/alien/Projects/research/sources/iq-sex-diff/nlsy97_piat_cat_pass.py#L282))
- Construct mismatch between standardized tests and school outputs. `HSLS` may be showing that GPA and course-linked outputs reward a different bundle than `X2TXMTSCOR`, not necessarily evaluative bias. The `NLSY97` `Math Knowledge` concentration points the same way. ([hsls_wedge_first_pass.py](/Users/alien/Projects/research/sources/iq-sex-diff/hsls_wedge_first_pass.py#L187), [nlsy97_piat_cat_cross_surface.tsv](/Users/alien/Projects/research/sources/iq-sex-diff/data/nlsy/nlsy97_piat_cat_cross_surface.tsv#L2))
- Transcript availability and persistence selection. The later `NLSY97` transcript models run on `n=2401` to `3957`, not the fixed `PIAT/CAT` overlap, so transcript persistence is not clean same-sample evidence. `HSLS` also conditions on transcript coverage plus complete transcript/test fields. ([nlsy97_piat_cat_transcript_models.tsv](/Users/alien/Projects/research/sources/iq-sex-diff/data/nlsy/nlsy97_piat_cat_transcript_models.tsv#L2), [hsls_wedge_first_pass.py](/Users/alien/Projects/research/sources/iq-sex-diff/hsls_wedge_first_pass.py#L87))
- Opportunity-set and placement heterogeneity. The `HSLS` underplacement pushback is too strong because `precalc_plus ~ female + baseline_math` rules out only one narrow story; it does not test school offerings, prior grades, recommendations, or within-school track rules. ([hsls_wedge_first_pass.py](/Users/alien/Projects/research/sources/iq-sex-diff/hsls_wedge_first_pass.py#L196), [iq-sex-differences-hsls-wedge-first-pass.md](/Users/alien/Projects/research/research/iq-sex-differences-hsls-wedge-first-pass.md#L131))
