## Hard constraints

1. Stay inside the repo’s truth-first constitution.
2. Avoid fake precision and double counting.
3. Prefer modules with identifiable local data hooks over vague social theory.
4. Treat category discipline as mandatory: legal low-skill, unauthorized, surge, and mixed flows are not interchangeable.

## Soft preferences

1. Favor questions that can be decomposed into measurable channels.
2. Favor questions that sharpen policy relevance rather than adding rhetoric.
3. Prefer modules that can later connect to the existing `DuckDB` warehouse.

1. Source everything.
2. Steel-man before criticizing.
3. Quantify when possible.
4. Name the frame.
5. Disconfirmation is mandatory.

## Knowledge injection

Tangential patterns injected before ideation:

1. `surge triage / crisis-care triggers`
   Source example: ASPR TRACIE hospital surge capacity collections and crisis-care trigger frameworks.
   Transfer: think in thresholds, trigger states, and escalation ladders rather than linear per-person averages.
2. `Swiss cheese risk management`
   Source example: layered-defense models where many weak institutional holes align before failure.
   Transfer: employer exploitation, court backlog, and enforcement failure may be multi-layer coordination problems, not single-channel costs.
3. `bank stress testing`
   Source example: Federal Reserve and Bank of England scenario stress tests.
   Transfer: test immigration-incidence claims under adverse local scenarios rather than only under average conditions.

3. treating housing as the only real omitted channel
4. turning “politics” into a vague cohesion slogan

## Evaluation criteria

Candidate ideas score higher if they:

1. open a new measurable branch
2. reduce an existing ambiguity in the repo
3. can be connected to current data assets or plausible next acquisitions
4. avoid obvious double counting with existing fiscal and housing work
